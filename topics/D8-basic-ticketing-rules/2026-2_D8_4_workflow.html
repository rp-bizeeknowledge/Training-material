Workflow: From Account Lookup to Solved Ticket

The Six Phases
PHASE 1

Locating the Order on DB

Follow these steps until you locate the customer's account. Be aware that authentication may be required:

Authentication not required:

  1. Copy and paste their email
  2. Use their phone number

Authentication required:

  1. Use their name
  2. Use the "Link to DB" (if available)
  3. Ask for the order number or company name

Once an order pulls up, make sure to verify the company name. Continue with assistance. If you cannot locate the order after any of these steps, ask the customer to check the initial emails from us to obtain the order number. They can call us back once they find it.

PHASE 2

Customer Service Job Aid

Cancellations and Refunds: Refer to the flowchart in the Knowledge Base.
Changes and Corrections to an Order: Refer to the Knowledge Base article on making changes on orders.
Escalations: Redirect the matter at hand to the corresponding department when the issue cannot be resolved by yourself. Refer to the escalation guidelines article in KB.
General Queries: Answer general queries for existing and new customers and provide guidance.
Order Issues: Explain the specific order issue. Refer to the Order Issues article in KB.
PHASE 3

Internal Notes and DB Comments

All agents are required to leave internal notes when closing or escalating a request on Zendesk. Escalations require different information annotated in internal comments; follow the escalation guidelines according to the corresponding department.

DB comments must be written as well, as they will contain requirements to facilitate the resolution of future requests from the same customer.

PHASE 4

Merging

On some occasions, merging tickets is required. Tickets are merged when there are several tickets to one query.

When to Merge a Ticket:

  • You should never merge your tickets into solved tickets unless it's a callback that was made prior to the issue being addressed at a later time.
  • If there are multiple tickets regarding the same matter, you must merge all tickets into the one you will be replying to, making sure that all queries are addressed (make sure that you are not merging your ticket into another untouched ticket).
  • You must merge tickets into yours when the query of their ticket is the same as your existing open or pending ticket. This prevents double escalations.

Merging 2 Tickets: Click on the 3 dots in the top right corner of the window. Once you see the drop-down menu, click on the option "Merge into another ticket". You'll be able to see a window requesting a ticket number. Keep in mind that the ticket number being requested is the one that you'll merge your current ticket into, so make sure that you input the correct number before clicking the "Merge" button. Make sure to deselect the checkboxes that say "User can see this comment". Once a ticket is merged, no one can unmerge it.

Merging Various Tickets from your Views: When merging various tickets from your views, select the tickets that will be merged into another ticket and click on "merge" at the bottom of the screen. Type in the ticket number you'll be merging into or select it from the list underneath the box, and click merge.

Merging Various Tickets from the Same User: Click on the tab showing the name of the user in the upper left-hand corner and select the tickets you will be merging. Do not select the ticket that the others will be merged into. Click on the down arrow on the black box in the upper right-hand corner and select "Merge tickets into another ticket...". Select the ticket to be merged into located underneath the box or type in the ticket number on the box, and click merge.

Keeping Our Records Updated

Zendesk is not automatically updated once a customer changes their contact information, which can cause user data to be out of date and inaccurate, or unavailable, leading to complications while trying to locate an account, contacting the customer by phone, or sending an email request.

Make sure to offer to update the user profile in Zendesk every time you notice a discrepancy against what's shown on DB/Bizee Tech, or if the customer has already informed you that their previous email or phone number is no longer being used, only if the caller/email sender has already been authenticated.

If the customer agrees: Make sure you send an email requesting written confirmation of the change before applying it to the account on DB/Bizee Tech.

If the customer declines: Change the ticket requester by entering the email address shown for the caller/email sender in DB/Bizee Tech.

Good Housekeeping Practices

Other scenarios that require you to update the User profile in Zendesk include:

Duplicate contact info: Watch for repeated email or phone entries. If duplicate details appear across profiles, confirm the correct account and keep Zendesk aligned with DB/Bizee Tech.
Missing names: Fill in incomplete contact names. When the customer confirms their identity, update missing first or last names so future agents can locate the record faster.
Outdated contact info: Offer to update stale phone or email details. If they reached out from a new contact method, ask whether they want it saved and update DB and Zendesk when confirmed.
PHASE 5

Ticket Submission & Statuses

There are only 4 ways to submit a ticket: Open, Pending, On-hold, and Solved.

Open

Submit as Open if a follow-up is required, but it is not an escalation.

Pending

Submit as Pending every time it's escalated.

On-Hold

Only to be used by supervisors or escalation departments. Agents should not use this submission label.

Solved

Submit as Solved when responding to the client, and no further action from the agent is required.

PHASE 6

Round-Robin Reassignment

Reassignment Policy: The round-robin system will automatically reassign open, pending, and on-hold status tickets when an agent is on vacation, sick leave, or has temporarily disabled their round-robin assignments for any reason.

Active Round-Robin: When the round-robin system is active, agents may receive open, pending, or on-hold tickets assigned to them.

Responsibility: Agents are required to manage any assigned tickets as if they were their own. Ensure to read the ticket details and respond promptly to customer inquiries.
