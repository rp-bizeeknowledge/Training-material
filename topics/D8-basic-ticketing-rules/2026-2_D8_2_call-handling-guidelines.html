Call Handling

Call opening · Follow-up rules · Voicemail · Dropped calls · User management · Hold · Away status · Missed calls
Call Opening Instructions

Thank the caller for contacting Bizee.com. Introduce yourself by name and offer to help. You can say it in your own words as long as it is clear, friendly, and helpful.

It is not required to use the caller's name from Zendesk. Only use it if you are sure how to pronounce it. You can ask for their name or leave it out.

Follow-Up Rules

If a follow-up or confirmation email is needed after a call, send it through the same ticket. Do not ask the customer to contact the Customer Support team.

Voicemail Guidelines

When you receive a voicemail ticket, call the customer back, even if they reached out from an international number.

1

Go to "Offline" status before proceeding.

2

Call the customer back.

3

If they don't answer, leave a voicemail with your name, inviting the customer to call us back if they need further assistance.

4

If you can't leave a voicemail, send an email, leave an internal note saying "Unable to LVM" and mark the ticket as Solved.

If you are calling an international number, it's important to select the outgoing number in Zendesk as "international calls".

Dropped-Call Guidelines

If a call drops for any reason (whether the connection was lost or the customer hung up before you removed them from hold), follow the steps below to ensure proper handling:

1

Go to "Offline" status before proceeding.

2

Call the customer back unless they clearly asked not to be contacted again before the call ended.

3

If they don't answer, leave a voicemail with your name, inviting the customer to call us back if they need further assistance.

4

If you can't leave a voicemail, send an email, leave an internal note saying "Unable to LVM" and mark the ticket as closed.

Managing Users in Zendesk

Some interactions may require updating the user's information in Zendesk or applying one-time changes.

Updating the Requester

Always update the requester to the email associated with the customer in our database. Do not use any other email as the requester.

Adding a Phone Number

If the phone number in the database doesn't match the number the customer is calling from, ask for their consent before updating it. If the number matches the database, no consent is needed to confirm it.

CCs Box Guidelines

Don't use the CCs box if: the request only contains a blank phone number, or there is no user profile associated with the contact.
Use the CCs box if: the customer has both an email and phone number on file, but wants the communication sent to a different email. If this is the case, inform them: "You will receive a copy of this communication at your primary email on file as well."

Email Consistency and Updates

Always try to keep the customer's primary email on file aligned with the primary contact email in the database. If you notice a mismatch, proactively offer to update their primary email to match their preference.

Primary vs. Secondary Emails in Zendesk

Zendesk doesn't send communications to secondary emails listed on file. If a customer wants to receive an email at both their primary and secondary address, you must manually add the secondary email to the CCs field located before the Assignee field in Zendesk.

Call Hold Handling

Calls may be placed on hold by either the agent or the customer. The following section covers how to manage common hold scenarios with consistency, professionalism, and minimal impact on the customer experience.

Agent-Initiated Holds

Agents may place a customer on hold when brief, focused time is required to retrieve information, review account details, consult a Supervisor, or complete a necessary action related to the call.

Ask for the customer's consent before placing them on hold by briefly explaining the reason for the hold.
Keep hold time under 3 minutes whenever possible. If additional time is needed, return to the line before the window expires and provide a brief status update.
When returning, thank the customer for waiting and confirm they are ready to continue.
Provide a concise summary of what was reviewed or confirmed during the hold and proceed with the interaction.

Do not place a customer on hold without explanation or consent.

Do not use holds to multitask or work on unrelated matters.

Do not extend holds beyond 3 minutes without checking back in.

Customer-Initiated Holds & Dead Air

Follow these steps when a customer requests to place you on hold, places you on hold without notice, or leaves extended dead air on the line:

Set clear expectations about hold times whenever possible by politely informing the customer that you can only remain on hold for 2 minutes.
If more than 2 minutes have elapsed, let the customer know and wait a few seconds for a response.
If the customer does not come back, you are authorized to release the call after saying: "I will need to release the call due to no interaction. Have a nice day!"
Document the outcome in the ticket's internal note if the call is released.

Do not end the call prematurely (i.e., right after the customer placed you on hold).

Do not remain on hold indefinitely without attempting to re-engage.

Do not end the call without a verbal notice.

Use of Away Status

By default, you have 1 minute of Away time between calls, as long as no calls are waiting in the queue. This time is intended to allow you to wrap up the previous interaction by leaving notes or updating the account. When call volume is high, and calls are waiting, this automatic Away time may be shortened or unavailable to keep calls moving.

Do not use Away Status to leave your station, or within the minutes leading up to, or right after a scheduled break or lunch. You are expected to remain available until your time off officially begins.

If you are working on a ticket between calls and need additional time, change your Status to Away for no more than 5 minutes, following these guidelines:

Notify your supervisor on Slack if you are handling a complex case or need assistance.
Only remain on Away if you are actively working on the ticket (e.g., reviewing the account, checking a Secretary of State website).
Do not use Away just to wait for a customer response or to ask a question.

You may place yourself on Away up to 5 minutes before the end of your shift, but not earlier than that.

Missed Calls

A missed call happens when:

The phone rings for 30 seconds without being answered
The customer hangs up before the 30 seconds are up

Agents must avoid missed calls.
