D4 · Section 04

Special Scenarios

EIN · Form 2553 · BOIR · VA · RA · SFB · Trademarks · BLRP · Kit · Domain · Bizee Tax · Payroll
Overview

Special procedures apply to certain Miscellaneous Product Orders, such as EIN Procurement, Miscellaneous Services including Registered Agent and Virtual Address subscriptions, and other co-sourced offerings, as outlined below.

4.1 Miscellaneous Products

In-House-Processed Orders that result in the delivery of a completed document

4.1.1 EIN as Add-On

Conditional

Rules:

  • Refunds are only applicable to Basic Orders with added EIN procurement.
  • Cancellation and refund is not available if we have obtained the EIN, if the fax application for the SS-4 has been sent, or if the EIN procurement is part of a Standard or Premium Order.

Steps:

  1. Go to the Transaction History Tab in DB/Bizee Tech.
  2. Issue a partial refund of $70 from the total amount for the order. Make sure to select "Other" from the Reason Dropdown options.

If the scenario you encounter is not mentioned above, make sure to escalate it by checking off the "EIN/SS4 Escalation" box via Zendesk.

4.1.2 EIN-Only (as Stand-Alone)

Conditional

Rules:

  • EIN-only orders can be cancelled and refunded exclusively while in Review Status, regardless of whether it pertains to a domestic or foreign applicant.
  • A refund is only available if no fax has been sent (if applying through an SS-4), and no EIN was generated through automation.
  • Do not cancel or refund if the order has been Shipped. (These orders will only go from Review to Shipped.)
  • If six months have passed since the customer initially signed the SS-4 form, the EIN has not been obtained, and the customer requests a refund or raises concerns about the delay: issue a refund for the EIN obtainment service fee, and escalate the case to the EIN department only if the customer confirms they would like us to submit additional procurement attempts.

If the scenario you encounter isn't mentioned above, escalate it by checking off the "EIN/SS4 Escalation" box in Zendesk. Inform the customer that you'll respond as soon as you receive a reply, which should be within the next 1 or 2 business days.

Steps:

  1. Place it on Hold and refresh DB/Bizee Tech to ensure there are no recent updates.
  2. Expand the Status dropdown in the panel on the left, then click the Cancel/Refund option.
  3. Leave a clear comment about the cancellation on DB/Bizee Tech, and an internal note on Zendesk.

4.1.3 Form 2553

Conditional

Rules:

  • Form 2553 orders can be cancelled and refunded exclusively while in Review Status.
  • Refunds are not available if the 2553 is part of a Main Order for an S-CORP, Standard and Premium packages for LLCs and Corps.
  • We can only cancel and refund if there is no fax receipt. Always go through the Comment Section and the Document Upload Tab to confirm.

Steps:

  1. Place it on Hold and refresh DB/Bizee Tech to ensure there are no recent updates.
  2. Expand the Status dropdown in the panel on the left, then click the Cancel/Refund option.
  3. Leave a clear comment about the cancellation in DB/Bizee Tech, and an internal note on Zendesk.

Special Case: Formation Packages — If Form 2553 is part of a formation package (e.g., Standard or Premium) for an LLC or Corporation, and the customer no longer wants it:

  1. Check the Document Escalation box.
  2. Submit as Pending, requesting removal of the form from the dashboard.

4.1.4 Business Contract Templates

Conditional

Rules:

  • No refunds are available if the templates were included in a package.
  • If they were purchased separately, visit the order's dashboard and click on the contract template section. Try and preview the PDF/DOCX. If the templates have not been downloaded, a warning message will appear — this informs the customer that these will not be refundable once they accept to continue. If the warning appears, you can continue with the cancellation and refund. If no warning appears and you are able to see the templates, then a cancellation and refund are not possible.

Steps:

  1. Go to the order's dashboard.
  2. If the warning appears, remove the Contract Templates from the list in DB/Bizee Tech, and provide the refund.
  3. Make sure to leave a clear comment about the cancellation in DB/Bizee Tech, and an internal note on Zendesk.

4.1.5 Beneficial Ownership Information Report (BOIR)

Conditional

Rules:

  • Refunds are available if the BOIR was not completed (Shipped) while the service was offered.
  • Do NOT offer BOIR refunds proactively.

Steps:

  1. Expand the Status dropdown in the panel on the left, then click the Cancel/Refund option.
  2. Leave a clear comment about the cancellation in DB/Bizee Tech, and an internal note on Zendesk.
4.2 Miscellaneous Services

In-House-Processed Orders that result in the ongoing fulfilment of a role or delivery of service

4.2.1 Virtual Address within a Main Order

Conditional

Rules:

  • Always verify VA usage — go through the company's Formation Documents and records on the State's Website to confirm whether the VA is listed as the company's, members' or directors' address. If listed: inform the customer that they need to file an Amendment to update the information with the state after cancellation. If not listed: confirm the new address in writing and update DB/Bizee Tech as applicable.
  • Agents may issue up to 3 months ($87 total) as a courtesy refund.
  • If the customer asks for more than the 3-month total, do not issue a refund. Tell the customer you will send the request for further review. In Zendesk, escalate the case by selecting your supervisor's name from the dropdown. Do not promise any refund amount until it is approved.
  • Always ask for the cancellation reason, and set the Ticket Type to Virtual Address, the Virtual Address Type to Cancellation, and select the most accurate reason from the Cancellation Reason dropdown before solving the ticket.

Steps:

  1. Go to the Virtual Address Tab in DB/Bizee Tech.
  2. Click the red Cancel button next to the active VA subscription to be cancelled. If there are multiple active subscriptions, make sure that the correct state is selected.
  3. If a refund is applicable, go to the Transaction History Tab and locate the $29 VA charge to be refunded. You may only issue refunds for the 3 most recent VA transactions.

4.2.3 Virtual Address as a Stand-Alone

Conditional

VA Child Orders will automatically create a Ghost Order (Parent Order). The Child Order will have "Transferred" as the status. Agents will need to apply these cancellations from the Ghost (Parent) Order.

Rules:

  • Always verify VA usage — check formation documents and state records to confirm if the VA is listed.
  • Verify if the order included filing an Amendment: if included and filed, cancel and refund VA fee only (up to 3 months). If included and not filed, cancel and refund in full. If not included, cancel and refund in full.
  • Agents may issue a refund for up to 3 months ($87 total) as a courtesy. If a higher refund is requested, escalate to your Supervisor.
  • Always ask for the cancellation reason and set the Ticket Type and Cancellation Reason fields before solving the ticket.

Steps:

  1. Go to the Subscriptions Tab for the Parent Order in DB/Bizee Tech.
  2. Click the red Cancel button next to the active VA subscription to be cancelled.
  3. If a refund is applicable, go to the Transaction History Tab, locate the applicable $29 VA charge and click Refund. You may only issue refunds for the 3 most recent VA transactions.

4.2.4 Registered Agent within a Main Order

Conditional
ℹ️

Note: When a Registered Agent service is cancelled, we stage the resignation but do not resign before the active term ends. Service continues until the expiration date, and resignation is triggered only after that date. This differs from other services (domains, email, virtual address), which cancel immediately.

Rules:

  • Always verify RA usage — check the company's records on the State's Website to confirm if we are listed as the company's RA. If listed: it is mandatory to inform the customer that they need to file a Change of Agent to update the information with the state after, to avoid losing good standing. If not listed: update DB/Bizee Tech with the new information.
  • Agents should only select "Client Cancelled the RA Service" in the cancellation reason field in DB/Bizee Tech while processing an RA refund.
  • You may only issue a refund for one year of service ($119) if requested within 3 months of the purchase/renewal.

Steps:

  1. Determine if a refund is applicable based on the General Rules above.
  2. Follow the correct procedure depending on the specific case.
Cancel and Refund (RA was not updated with SOS)

Cancel the service directly on the Transaction History tab by selecting refund on the latest Registered Agent charge.

Cancel and Refund (RA was updated with SOS)

Cancel the service directly on the Transaction History tab by selecting refund on the latest Registered Agent charge. Set the registered agent to "no agent" and update the information in DB/Bizee Tech to match that of the Secretary of State's website.

Cancel No Refund (RA was not updated with SOS)

Set the registered agent in Stage for Resignation.

Cancel No Refund (RA was updated with SOS)

Set the registered agent to "no agent" and update the information in DB/Bizee Tech to match that of the Secretary of State's website.

After cancelling, if the company is active on the SOS website and we are still listed as the RA, it is mandatory that you inform the customer that we will be resigning as their Registered Agent, and unless they update the information with the state, their company can get administratively dissolved.

Multiple Years Refund

If a customer reinstated multiple years' worth of the Registered Agent subscription in a single payment (for example, the renewal fee paid is $238, $357, $476, $595, etc.) and is requesting a refund, follow these guidelines:

  • Escalate the client's request with your supervisor to find out if a refund applies, and if so, how much will be refunded (every case varies, that's why it's necessary to consult with a supervisor). The escalation will be made on Zendesk by selecting your supervisor from the supervisor escalation dropdown.
  • If the supervisor determines a partial refund applies, you will confirm the contact address we have on file. This is because our system (DB/Bizee Tech) won't let us provide partial refunds for registered agent renewal charges, so we have to refund with a check.
  • Escalate the check refund request to the supervisor using the supervisor escalation dropdown.
Reverting a Registered Agent Cancellation

If the customer retracts after cancelling the Registered Agent and wishes to keep the service active, you can reinstate the subscription on their behalf if the conditions below are met. This also applies if the customer cancels the subscription, but they still want to take advantage of the subscription until the expiration date.

This procedure applies ONLY if:

  • The Registered Agent was marked as "Staged for Resignation",
  • No refund was given, and
  • The service was not expired (confirm this by checking the expiration date in DB/Bizee Tech).

If the conditions are met:

  1. Inform the customer that their subscription will be reactivated, and to avoid future charges, they need to contact us before the renewal date so we can cancel the service for them.
  2. If the customer agrees, change the Registered Agent from "Staged for Resignation" to "Yes" on the Registered Agent tab of DB/Bizee Tech.

In all other cases, the customer will be able to reinstate the subscription from their dashboard.

4.2.5 Registered Agent as a Stand-Alone

Conditional

RA Child Orders will automatically create a Ghost Order (Parent Order). The Child Order will have "Transferred" as the status. Cancellations have to be done from the Ghost (Parent) Order.

Rules:

  • Always verify RA usage — check the company's records on the State's Website. If listed: inform the customer that they need to file a Change of Agent (CoA) to update the information with the state after cancellation. If not listed: set the Registered Agent to "no agent" and update DB/Bizee Tech to match the Secretary of State's website.
  • Verify if the order included filing a Change of Agent: if included and filed, cancel and refund RA fee only. If included and not filed, cancel and refund the order in full. If not included, cancel and refund the order in full.
  • You may only issue a refund for one year of service if requested within 3 months of the purchase/renewal. If a higher refund is requested, escalate to your Supervisor.

Steps:

  1. Go to the Agent Tab for the Parent Order in DB/Bizee Tech.
  2. Click the blue Edit button and set the Registered Agent Service field to "Staged for Resignation".
  3. If a refund is applicable, go to the Transaction History Tab, locate the applicable RA charge and click Refund.

4.2.6 State Filings Bundle

Conditional

Rules:

  • You are authorized to issue a refund only if the customer has NOT rendered any Miscellaneous Orders after subscribing to the bundle.
  • Refunds are only available within 30 days from the original charge.
  • If a customer requests a refund but has a Miscellaneous Order that used the bundle: a refund is allowed only if the Miscellaneous Order was previously cancelled. If the customer wants to cancel the Miscellaneous Order, follow the corresponding cancellation procedure if applicable and then process the State Filings Bundle refund.
  • Cancelling with no refund is available; this will deactivate the automatic renewal, and the service will remain active until one year has passed from the day the customer signed up for the service.

Steps:

  1. Go to the Subscriptions Tab in DB/Bizee Tech.
  2. Click the red Cancel button next to the active State Filings subscription to be cancelled.
  3. If a refund is applicable, go to the Transaction History Tab, locate the applicable $99 charge and click Refund.
4.3 Co-Sourced Products

4.3.1 Trademarks

Escalate First

Rules:

  • Trademarks ordered after August 1, 2022, are handled by Unikel Law.
  • Trademarks never go to Forwarded Status. Once in Processing, the team is most likely about to contact the customer for their request.
  • If the Customer claims they have not been contacted in more than two weeks, assess whether they want to move forward with the order and escalate accordingly.
  • If only a partial refund is authorized, they will specify the amount in the side conversation.
  • Always inform the customer that you have escalated the request and keep them updated throughout the process.

Steps:

  1. Use the macro "Trademark Escalation - Side Conversation - Unikel Law" to create a side conversation with the provider, and fill out the requested information to confirm if cancellation is possible.
  2. Once authorization is received, expand the Status dropdown on the left panel in DB/Bizee Tech, then click the Cancel option.
  3. Go to the Transaction History, locate the corresponding charge, and process the refund for the authorized amount.

4.3.2 Business License Research Package (BLRP)

Escalate First

Rules:

  • BLRP cancellation & Refund requests have to be authorized by Wolters Kluwer.
  • Refunds are available only if the research has not been conducted or delivered within the promised timeframe.

Standalone Orders:

  1. Use the macro "Wolters Kluwer Email - BLRP" to create a side conversation with the provider.
  2. Once authorization is received, expand the Status dropdown on the left panel in DB/Bizee Tech, then click the Cancel option.
  3. Go to the Transaction History, locate the corresponding $99 charge.

Included in a Main Order:

  1. If two weeks have passed since the Date of Formation, and the BLRP has not been delivered, use the macro "Wolters Kluwer Email - BLRP" to create a side conversation with the provider.
  2. Once authorization is received, go to the Transaction History and issue a $99 refund from the total order amount.

4.3.3 Business Formation Kit

Escalate First

Rules:

  • Cancellation & Refund requests have to be authorized by Fastkits.
  • Refunds are available if the order is under review, processing or hold status.

Steps:

  1. Use the macro "Kit Cancellation Email-Fastkit Email" to create a side conversation with the provider.
  2. Once authorization is received, expand the Status dropdown on the left panel in DB/Bizee Tech, then click the Cancel option.
  3. Go to the Transaction History Tab, locate the applicable $99 charge and click Refund.
4.4 Co-Sourced Services

4.4.1 Domain & Email

Conditional

Rules:

  • Refunds only apply when the Domain and Email feature was renewed less than 3 business days ago.
  • If the Domain and Email feature was renewed more than 3 business days ago, you can cancel the service, but do not refund the renewal fee.
  • You can cancel the Domain & Email subscription at any time. Access to all domain and email features stops right after the cancellation is processed; always let customers know this before cancelling, so they have a chance to run a backup if needed.

If unsure on how to proceed, escalate your ticket to the Domain and Email Department through the checkbox in Zendesk.

Steps:

  1. Send the "Domain and Email – Cancellation Request" macro if assisting via email, or use it as a guide to explain domain registration limitations and immediate email service disruption. Once written or verbal confirmation is obtained, proceed to the next step.
  2. Go to the Subscriptions Tab in DB/Bizee Tech.
  3. Click the red Cancel button next to the active Domain & Business Email subscription to be cancelled. If there are multiple active subscriptions, make sure that the correct domain is selected.
  4. If a refund is applicable, go to the Transaction History Tab and issue the refund from the corresponding D&E charge.

4.4.2 Bizee Tax

Conditional

Rules:

  • This subscription can be cancelled by the customer through their Dashboard. They can do so by going to the Taxes tab and scrolling down to view the subscription section.
  • Refunds can only be issued for the most recent transaction, up to one month total.
  • Customers have 30 natural days from the date of the charge to request a refund.
  • If the cancellation request comes through email, or if the customer prefers that you handle the cancellation over the phone, do not cancel it from the dashboard. Agent cancellations must be done following the steps below.

Steps:

  1. Go to the Subscriptions Tab in DB/Bizee Tech.
  2. Click the red Cancel button next to the active Bizee Tax subscription to be cancelled.
  3. If a refund is applicable, go to the Transaction History Tab and issue the refund from the corresponding Bizee Tax charge.
