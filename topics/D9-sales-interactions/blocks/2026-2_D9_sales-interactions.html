D9 — Sales Interactions

Sales
Interactions

Every inbound contact is an opportunity to provide a solution
The Mindset

We approach every inbound email or phone call as an opportunity to provide a solution to someone's needs.

Anyone who reaches out to us is treated as a valued customer, even if no formal business relationship has begun yet. Our goal is to deliver tailored, helpful, and clear support from the very first contact. This guide establishes how agents should approach conversations with potential customers in a way that is accurate, and aligned with what we offer.

What to Avoid
✗

Do not dismiss or shortcut an interaction because the person is not yet a paying customer.

✗

Do not provide advice, interpretations, or instructions outside the services we offer.

✗

Do not default to saying "we cannot help" or "we don't do that" without first understanding the caller's actual goal.

✗

Do not speculate, guess, or offer unofficial solutions.

How to Approach
1

Open with the Right Focus

Start the interaction with an open mind. Begin by focusing on what the person is trying to accomplish rather than on whether they already have an order, payment, or account on file.

2

Listen, Acknowledge, and Clarify

Listen carefully and acknowledge the request. Many callers are unfamiliar with how our services work or with the terminology they are using. It is the agent's role to bring structure and clarity to the conversation.

Maintain a neutral, informative tone throughout the interaction. The goal is to create understanding, not urgency. A well-guided conversation should feel supportive and genuine, regardless of whether the person chooses to move forward.

3

Educate on Our Services

Use the conversation to identify the underlying objective behind the question. Callers often describe a task, a problem, or an end result without knowing which service actually addresses it.

Remain patient, and if you need additional information, ask probing questions and translate the caller's goal into the services we provide. Once the goal is clear, explain the relevant service at a high level and outline how the process generally works.

When we do not offer exactly what the caller is asking for, shift the focus to the closest outcome we can support. Clearly explain what we do offer, how it differs, and how it may still help them move forward. If no product or service we offer could help them reach that outcome, kindly thank them for their time and let them know we don't currently support a solution for them.

Closing the Interaction

End every sales interaction with clarity. The person should leave the conversation understanding what we offer, what applies to their situation, and what their options are.

✓ What a Successful Interaction Looks Like

A successful interaction is one where the caller feels informed, respected, and invited to conduct business with us — even if no immediate action is taken.
