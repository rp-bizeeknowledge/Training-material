STATE SPECIFICS · Topic 03

Texas Comptroller

Specific procedures to update information in Texas and obtain a Webfile number
Overview

In Texas, the Secretary of State handles the formation and public filing of business entities, whereas the Comptroller handles franchise tax, accounting functions, and maintains the tax records for each entity.

Because of this, updating business information in Texas may require different processes depending on which agency manages the specific data. Use these guidelines to identify whether the customer needs to file an amendment with the Secretary of State or make the update on their own with the Texas Comptroller.

Which Agency Handles What

Secretary of State

  • Company Name
  • Members'/Directors' Names and Addresses
  • Registered Agent

Texas Comptroller

  • Company's Mailing Address
  • Webfile Number
Mailing Address

By default, when we register a Texas company, we list our Registered Agent address as the mailing address. This ensures we receive the Comptroller's welcome letter, which contains the Webfile Number needed for future annual reports. Once received, we upload the letter to the customer's dashboard under State Notifications so they don't miss it. Afterward, customers may update the mailing address directly with the Texas Comptroller if they prefer correspondence sent elsewhere.

Webfile Number

This is a 6-digit alphanumeric number that usually starts with RT, XT, or FQ, and is used to access the Comptroller's online filing access. It gets assigned to a business along with the taxpayer number immediately after its registration in the Secretary of State's records, and is provided through a "Welcome Letter" that is mailed out by the Comptroller a couple of weeks after the company has been formed.

Example of the welcome letter from the Texas Comptroller with the Webfile number
Webfile Letter Missing

If the customer reports not receiving the Webfile number letter in their Virtual Mailbox after formation, confirm on the Secretary of State website whether our Registered Agent address is listed as the company's Mailing Address.

Address is listed

1. If more than 2 weeks have passed since formation, tell the customer that the case will be reviewed with the Virtual Address team and that an update will be provided by email once a response is received.

2. Escalate the case by checking the RA/VA Mail Escalations checkbox. Include the order number, company name, and a brief description of the issue.

Address is not listed

1. Explain to the customer that the Comptroller would have mailed the Webfile letter to the mailing address reflected in the state records.

2. If the customer did not receive the letter after checking the mailing address, suggest contacting the Texas Comptroller to see if they can provide the Webfile number over the phone or resend the welcome letter.

Updating the Comptroller's Records

If a customer wants to update the mailing address after the company has been formed, they must do so directly with the Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts. As a third party, we are not authorized to make changes to the information recorded by the Comptroller on a company's behalf.

Online: comptroller.texas.gov/web-forms/franchise/changeaddress/
Phone: (800) 442-3453

When requested, we send our customers an email to provide detailed instructions on completing these updates. The customer will be required to provide their Webfile Number to complete the address change.

Sales Tax Registration

In simple terms, sales tax registration in Texas means signing up with the state so you can collect sales tax from customers and send it to Texas. If you sell taxable products or services in Texas, the state wants you to act as the tax collector.

Once you register, Texas gives you a sales tax permit. That permit allows you to charge sales tax on taxable sales, file sales tax reports, and send the collected tax to the state. You cannot legally collect Texas sales tax without this permit.

You usually must register if you sell physical products in Texas, sell taxable digital products, provide taxable services in Texas, have a business location, employees, or inventory in Texas, or sell online and ship to Texas customers (even from another state, once you pass the economic nexus threshold).

Sales Tax Registration Scenarios

Local store: You open a clothing boutique in Austin. Clothing is taxable. You must register for Texas sales tax before selling anything.

Online seller: You run an online store from Florida and ship products to Texas customers. If your Texas sales exceed the state threshold, you must register and collect Texas sales tax.

Service business: You offer website design. Most web design services are not taxable in Texas. If you only sell non-taxable services, you may not need to register.

Digital products: You sell downloadable templates to Texas customers. Digital goods are taxable in Texas, so registration is required.

After you register, you add Texas sales tax to eligible sales, file monthly, quarterly, or annual reports, and remit the tax collected to the state.

Registering too late or collecting tax without a permit can lead to penalties. Register first, then collect.

Businesses that Usually Do NOT Need to Register

Selling non-taxable services only: If you offer non-taxable services, registration usually does not apply (such as graphic design, marketing consulting, business coaching, writing or editing services).

Selling to customers outside Texas only: You sell physical products but never ship to Texas and have no Texas customers. No Texas sales means no Texas Sales Tax Registration.

Occasional or isolated sale: You sell used office equipment once and do not operate a retail business. One-off or casual sales usually do not trigger registration.

Nonprofits selling only exempt items: A nonprofit that only collects donations or sells exempt fundraising items. If the sales are exempt, registration may not apply.

Businesses below economic nexus with no Texas presence: An out-of-state online business with very low or no Texas sales. If you do not meet the Texas economic nexus threshold and have no physical presence, registration does not apply.

If you are not selling taxable goods or taxable services to Texas customers, Sales Tax Registration usually does not apply.

Things People Often Think Apply But Do Not

Having an LLC or corporation alone: Entity type does not automatically trigger sales tax. What you sell matters.

Having a website: A website alone does not require registration. Taxable sales to Texas customers do.

Charging invoices instead of checkout sales: How you bill does not change tax rules.

Any queries about the Sales Tax Registration should be escalated to your supervisor for review.

How to File

The option to file the Sales Tax Registration is available in the customer's dashboard, under the State and IRS filings tab. This is only available for Texas companies.

Afterward, they will be able to review the necessary information to place the order. Customers will complete all the required fields with their information to successfully continue placing the order.

Once the boxes are filled out, they will be able to submit the payment, and the order will be created and sent to our database.

Once the order is submitted, the information will be processed by our Filing team, and the information will be forwarded to the state. The customer should receive their registration in between 2-3 weeks (this is just an estimated time frame; it is not an actual delivery date provided to customers).
