Annual Report Access

Delaware · Pennsylvania · Texas · Florida · California
Overview

Each state has its own rules and procedures for handling Annual Report filings. In some states, it has a different name, different deadlines depending on the entity type, or may include the payment of state taxes or fees. Understanding these specific requirements is crucial for providing accurate support to our customers and ensuring their businesses remain in good standing.

Delaware

In Delaware, the Annual Report is called Franchise Tax Report, and it's due on June 1 for LLCs and March 1 for Corporations and Nonprofits. It has the following particularities:

Reporting Period

As opposed to other states, Delaware's Franchise Tax Report reflects income data from the previous tax year, not the current. Take for example:

A Corp formed December 5, 2025
  • First report is due March 1, 2026
  • Report will cover the period from December 5 to December 31, 2025
  • Dashboard will show "2026 Annual Report" but PDF file will reflect 2025 activity
Corporation Tax Calculation

Corporations in Delaware have to file an Annual Report based on their authorized shares. When a customer places an order, they will be charged the state fee plus our filing fee, and, depending on how their company is structured, additional fees may be required.

Authorized Shares Tax Amount
5,000 shares or less (minimum tax) $175
5,001 – 10,000 shares $250
Each additional 10,000 shares or portion thereof +$85
Maximum annual tax $200,000
Tax calculations
  • A corporation with 10,005 shares authorized pays $335 ($250 plus $85).
  • A corporation with 100,000 shares authorized pays $1,015 ($250 plus $765 [$85 × 9]).
Internal Handling

Clients might get a pending hold if they input too many shares. This is done to confirm that they want to report that number of shares and that they understand the consequences of it.

If additional fees are required, they will receive a Pending Issue. They will have to provide the information to the Order Issue team to continue with the filing.

If the customer has to pay less than the initial amount, a refund will be applicable to the order. You can confirm this information by reviewing the Annual Report document on Document Upload. There will be a note on DB/Bizee Tech with the refund information.

Pennsylvania

The first annual report in Pennsylvania is especially significant because it is the first state-issued document that publicly reflects the names of the entity's Members, Directors, or Officers. This should be carefully considered when assisting customers, as the required steps to add, remove, or update these individuals depend on the entity type and whether or not the report has been filed.

LLCs

Articles of Organization list the Members as Organizers, including the names and addresses provided during the order process. Once the first Annual Report is filed, the members will show as Governors with only their names. Organizer's addresses cannot be changed with the state, but updating the members' addresses in the internal documents is possible.

Always check if the first Annual Report has been filed when handling a customer's request to update membership information for a Pennsylvania LLC, and follow the steps below:

Florida

Florida's Annual Report requirements significantly influence our high season. In 2025, we received over 6,000 Florida Annual Report orders between March and May, as corporations, limited liability companies, and nonprofit organizations are required to file an annual report each year by May 1 to maintain active status.

Because the state enforces this deadline strictly, and the Annual Reports affect good standing and public records, familiarity with Florida's regulation is essential to provide efficient and accurate support.

Fines and Penalties

Reports can be filed beginning on January 1, but filing after May 1 triggers a $400 late fee and may ultimately lead to administrative dissolution if not filed before the third Friday in September. An extra $200 fine is added if this happens.

Internal Handling

The late fee is automatically added at checkout when the filing is close to the May 1st deadline (approximately three days prior). The fee cannot be removed at checkout, but can be refunded if ultimately not needed.

You can only process the refund if the Filing Department posted a DB Comment on the order authorizing the specific amount.

If applicable, you have to process the refund from the Transaction History tab, selecting "Other" as the refund reason, and typing "Late Fee Refund" in the description field.

Texas

In Texas, the Secretary of State handles the formation and public filing of business entities, whereas the Comptroller handles franchise tax, accounting functions, and maintains the tax records for each entity. Because of this, updating business information in Texas may require different processes depending on which agency manages the specific data.

Which Agency Handles What

Secretary of State

  • Company Name
  • Members'/Directors' Names and Addresses
  • Registered Agent

Comptroller

  • Company's Mailing Address
Mailing Address

By default, when we register a Texas company, we list our Registered Agent address as the mailing address. This ensures we receive the Comptroller's welcome letter, which contains the Webfile Number needed for future annual reports. Once received, we upload the letter to the customer's dashboard under State Notifications so they don't miss it. Afterward, customers may update the mailing address directly with the Texas Comptroller if they prefer correspondence sent elsewhere.

Webfile Number

This is a 6-digit alphanumeric number that usually starts with RT, XT, or FQ, and is used to access the Comptroller's online filing access. It gets assigned to a business along with the taxpayer number immediately after its registration in the Secretary of State's records, and is provided through a "Welcome Letter" that is mailed out by the Comptroller a couple of weeks after the company has been formed.

Updating the Comptroller's Records

If a customer wants to update the mailing address after the company has been formed, they must do so directly with the Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts. As a third party, we are not authorized to make changes to the information recorded by the Comptroller on a company's behalf.

Online: comptroller.texas.gov/web-forms/franchise/changeaddress/

Use the macro "Texas Comptroller Mailing Address Change" to provide customers with detailed instructions on completing these updates. The customer will be required to provide their Webfile Number to complete the address change.

Sales Tax Registration

In simple terms, sales tax registration in Texas means signing up with the state so you can collect sales tax from customers and send it to Texas. If you sell taxable products or services in Texas, the state wants you to act as the tax collector.

What registration actually does: Once you register, Texas gives you a sales tax permit. That permit allows you to charge sales tax on taxable sales, file sales tax reports, and send the collected tax to the state. You cannot legally collect Texas sales tax without this permit.

Who needs to register: You usually must register if you sell physical products in Texas, sell taxable digital products, provide taxable services in Texas, have a business location, employees, or inventory in Texas, or sell online and ship to Texas customers (even from another state, once you pass the economic nexus threshold).

Sales tax registration scenarios

Local store: You open a clothing boutique in Austin. Clothing is taxable. You must register for Texas sales tax before selling anything.

Online seller: You run an online store from Florida and ship products to Texas customers. If your Texas sales exceed the state threshold, you must register and collect Texas sales tax.

Service business: You offer website design. Most web design services are not taxable in Texas. If you only sell non-taxable services, you may not need to register.

Digital products: You sell downloadable templates to Texas customers. Digital goods are taxable in Texas, so registration is required.

What happens after registration: You add Texas sales tax to eligible sales, file monthly, quarterly, or annual reports, and remit the tax collected to Texas.

Registering too late or collecting tax without a permit can lead to penalties. Register first, then collect. Any queries about the Sales Tax Registration should be escalated to your supervisor for review.

Businesses that usually do NOT need to register
1

Selling only non-taxable services: e.g., graphic design, marketing consulting, business coaching, writing or editing services. If you only sell these services and no taxable products, registration usually does not apply.

2

Selling to customers outside Texas only: you sell physical products but never ship to Texas and have no Texas customers. No Texas sales means no Texas sales tax registration.

3

Occasional or isolated sales: you sell used office equipment once and do not operate a retail business. One-off or casual sales usually do not trigger registration.

4

Nonprofits selling only exempt items: a nonprofit that only collects donations or sells exempt fundraising items. If the sales are exempt, registration may not apply.

5

Businesses below economic nexus with no Texas presence : an out-of-state online business with very low or no Texas sales. If you do not meet the Texas economic nexus threshold and have no physical presence, registration does not apply.

Things people often think apply but do not: Having an LLC or corporation alone does not automatically trigger sales tax : what you sell matters. Having a website alone does not require registration. Charging invoices instead of checkout sales does not change tax rules.

If you are not selling taxable goods or taxable services to Texas customers, sales tax registration usually does not apply.

Annual Report (Public Information & Franchise Tax Report)

In Texas, the Annual Report includes two filings: the Franchise Tax Report and the Public Information Report (PIR). The Franchise Tax Report determines whether the business owes franchise tax based on its revenue, while the PIR updates the state's records with current ownership, officer, and registered agent details. Bizee handles both filings whenever a customer orders an Annual Report.

All entity types are required to file their Annual Report each year with the Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts. Both filings are due on May 15, even if no tax is owed. Failure to file may result in the forfeiture of the company's right to do business in Texas.

Revenue Threshold: Bizee does not allow Annual Report orders in Texas for businesses reporting revenue of $2.47 million or more. Customers who enter an amount equal to or above this threshold in the order form will see an error screen indicating that the service cannot be processed through our platform.

California

In California, the Annual Report is called the Statement of Information (SOI). Every California entity must file an initial SOI within 90 days of formation, as a requirement to maintain good standing.

After the initial filing, LLCs and NFPs are required to file the SOI every two years, while corporations do it every year. The filing window for all entities lasts 6 months and ends on the last day of the company's formation anniversary month.

Franchise Tax Board

The Franchise Tax Board (FTB) handles California business taxes. Most companies formed, registered, or doing business in California must pay a minimum Franchise Tax of $800 each year.

For LLCs, the first $800 payment is due by the 15th day of the 4th month after formation (the formation month counts as month one).

Corporations don't pay the $800 minimum tax in the first taxable year, and it typically begins in the second taxable year.

Nonprofit corporations may qualify for tax exemption at a state level. If the tax-exempt status is granted, the organization is generally not required to pay the Franchise Tax.

December Order Holds for Corporations

If a C Corporation order is placed in early December, the Filing Department will place the order on hold to notify the customer of Franchise Tax Payment implications. The FTB will charge an $800 Franchise Tax to all companies formed before December 17.
