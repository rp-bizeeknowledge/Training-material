California Franchise Tax Board

Common customer questions and internal handling
Overview

In California, the Secretary of State handles business filings like the Statement of Information, while the Franchise Tax Board (FTB) manages business taxes, including the required $800 minimum franchise tax.

These are separate requirements handled by different agencies. This article covers what the FTB requires and how to handle customer requests related to it. For Statement of Information filing details, see AR Special Scenarios.

Which Agency Handles What

Secretary of State

  • Statement of Information (Annual Report)
  • Amendments to company name, members, managers, and Business Purpose
  • Change of Registered Agent

Franchise Tax Board

  • $800 minimum franchise tax
  • State income tax returns (e.g., Forms 568, 100, and 199)
  • State tax-exempt status applications for Nonprofits (NFPs)
  • Final Tax Return for Entity Dissolutions
Franchise Tax Board

The Franchise Tax Board (FTB) handles California business taxes. Most companies formed, registered, or doing business in California must pay a minimum Franchise Tax of $800 each year.

LLCs
  • First $800 payment is due by the 15th day of the 4th month after formation (the formation month counts as month one).
    • Example: An LLC formed on March 13 must pay by June 15. After that, the $800 are due every year by April 15.
  • Additional LLC Fee: If a California LLC's income exceeds $250,000, the FTB will require an additional fee (typically due June 15).
C Corps
S Corps
  • No $800 minimum tax in the first taxable year.
  • Still required to file a tax return with the FTB.
  • The $800 tax typically begins in the second taxable year.
NFPs
  • May apply directly with the FTB for tax-exempt status.
  • If approved, generally no franchise tax is required.
  • Bizee does not file the state tax-exempt application.
Franchise Tax Payment Assistance Requests

If Bizee is the Registered Agent for a California entity, we receive the initial FTB payment notice. Customers can find this notice in their Virtual Mailbox labeled as a "State Notification" and may contact us after receiving it.

Bizee does not process Franchise Taxes.

The FTB is a separate agency from the Secretary of State, and we do not support submitting tax payments on a customer's behalf.

When handling these requests:

  • Direct the customer to the FTB website for payment and account access: ftb.ca.gov
  • Provide the FTB phone number: (800) 852-5711
  • Recommend a CPA or tax professional for any tax-related advice
December Order Holds for Corporations

If a C Corporation order is placed in early December, the Filing Department will place the order on hold to notify the customer of Franchise Tax Payment implications. The FTB will charge an $800 Franchise Tax to all companies formed before December 17th.

  • If the customer decides to continue and incorporate before 12/17, they will owe the current year's Franchise Tax.
  • If the customer instead chooses a future effective date of December 17, they will only be required to pay the Franchise Tax corresponding to the following year.
