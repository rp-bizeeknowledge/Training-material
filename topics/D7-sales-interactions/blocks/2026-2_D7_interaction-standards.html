5.1 — Email Communication

A thoughtful, clear email solves problems, eases frustration, and builds trust. Let's make each one count.

1

Understand

Before replying, take time to fully understand the customer's concern. Ask probing questions if needed, avoid assumptions, and investigate using internal tools. Use screenshots when helpful.

2

Authenticate

In order to grant access to account information and take action like cancellations or changes, follow the authentication process outlined in Section 4.

3

Respond

Reply within 4 hours of ticket assignment or your shift start time. Fast responses show professionalism and care.

4

Proofread

Ensure proper grammar, punctuation, and spelling. No more than 2 small errors per message.

5

Mind the Tone

Be friendly, respectful, and clear. Show empathy and match urgency — never use slang, blame, robotic phrasing, ALL CAPS, or emojis.

6

Maintain Confidence

Use direct, informed language. Avoid uncertain phrases like "I think" or "maybe".

7

Personalise

Start the first email with a greeting and the customer's name. Use your Zendesk signature to close. Skip greetings in follow-ups within the same thread as long as they are not more than 24 hours old.

8

Format

Use short paragraphs and bullet points to make content easy to scan and understand.

9

If Using Macros

Always insert the customer's name. Rephrase for smoother flow. Trim any irrelevant or filler content.

10

Ending the Thread

You can stop replying when: the customer replies with "thank you" and no further action is required; you've fully answered their question or resolved the issue; the customer does not respond after a follow-up; the issue has been escalated or transferred; or the concern has already been addressed in another ticket or phone call.

5.2 — Annotation Rules

All customer interactions must be clearly and consistently documented. This ensures accurate tracking of each case and enables effective, informed support. Documentation is maintained using two primary tools: Zendesk and DB.

Requirements
Every time you interact with a customer, leave a record in both Zendesk and DB (if an existing account was pulled up).
If additional, interdepartmental support is required, add an Internal Note with all relevant details so the reviewing team can clearly understand the situation and resolve it efficiently.

With a User ID — What to Include

  • Internal Notes: Order number, short summary of what the customer needs, and how we helped them.
  • DB Comments: Ticket number, short summary of the customer's request, and the solution provided.

Without a User ID — What to Include

  • Internal Notes only: Short description of the customer's request and the solution. No DB Comments needed because there's no user ID or order number.
Best Practices
Always use the "Internal Notes" section for writing down things, not for public responses.
If you accidentally respond publicly, apologize to the customer if needed, ask for help if the customer is upset, and explain the mistake in an Internal Note.
Make sure the information in Internal Notes and DB Comments is the same.
Write clearly and professionally, avoid slang, and use bullet points for better clarity.
The way you write should match the situation, especially if a customer is upset or the situation is serious.
5.3 — The (Mis)use of AI

ChatGPT and the embedded AI writing tools in Zendesk help agents write faster and with more confidence. They are especially useful when an agent needs to draft an email quickly, tighten language, or get a quick proofread before sending a response. These tools reduce the time spent thinking through sentence structure or tone, which lets agents focus on helping customers instead of wrestling with wording.

Sometimes agents copy the AI's suggestion into a ticket but forget to delete phrases like "ChatGPT said." This usually happens when someone is moving fast or switching between windows. It is an easy mistake, but it can confuse customers or reveal internal tools that are not meant to appear in replies.

The goal is to use AI as a writing assistant, not as part of the message itself. It can save time, sharpen communication, and help maintain a consistent voice, but agents should always review the text before sending it to make sure no AI prompts or leftover markers remain.

⚠️

We expect every agent to proofread their message at least twice before sending it. A quick reread helps catch leftover AI markers, missing details, or tone issues that can change how a customer understands the message. Never send a reply you have not taken the time to read from start to finish. Quality and accuracy come first, even when working quickly.

5.4 — Missed Calls & Callback Requests

Customers may contact us outside business hours, get disconnected during a call, or request a callback if they cannot remain on hold. To ensure excellent service and accurate documentation, follow the procedures below when handling missed calls or callback request tickets.

If a Call Drops Mid-Conversation
1

Go Offline

Set your status to Offline before proceeding with any follow-up action.

2

Attempt a Callback

Call the customer back unless they clearly asked not to be contacted again before the call ended.

3

If Unable to Connect

Leave a voicemail with your name and invite the customer to call us back if they need further assistance.

4

If Voicemail Cannot Be Left

In Zendesk, send an email, leave an internal note saying "Unable to LVM" and mark the ticket as Solved.

📌

These same steps also apply when a voicemail ticket has been assigned to you. You can call both U.S. and international numbers. If a number isn't supported, Zendesk will show a notification which you can screenshot and leave as part of the interaction's Internal Note.
