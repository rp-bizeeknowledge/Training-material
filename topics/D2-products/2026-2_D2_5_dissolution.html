Dissolution

State filing that formally terminates a business entity's legal existence, wraps up its financial obligations and closes its public record
What It Is

A Dissolution refers to the official paperwork required to terminate the existence of a business entity. This process formally notifies the Secretary of State that the company is no longer active, and it helps the business avoid future tax obligations, compliance requirements, or penalties. Updating Company Status in the DB

If you find a company listed as Dissolved, Delinquent, Administratively Dissolved or Revoked on the Secretary of State's website, you must update the company's status in the database (DB) as follows:

State Shows Update DB To
Revoked / Delinquent / Administratively Dissolved "Delinquent/Revoked"
Voluntarily Dissolved/Terminated "Dissolved"
Whenever you update a company's status:
1

Go to the Secretary of State's website and locate the official listing of the company's status.

2

Take a screenshot showing the current status.

3

Upload the screenshot to Internal Storage as documentation and backup for the update you're making in DB.

Companies that were administratively dissolved by the state must NOT be marked as "Dissolved." This status is reserved only for companies that have voluntarily dissolved. Always differentiate between voluntary and involuntary dissolution.

Foreign Qualification Dissolutions

Dissolutions for Foreign Qualifications are called withdrawals. A withdrawal is required when a company wants to certify that it is voluntarily terminating its registration as a foreign entity in that State. Bizee does not offer this service.
