Certificate of Good Standing

A state-issued certification verifying that a business is active and compliant with all filing requirements
What It Is

A Certificate of Good Standing (COGS) is an official document issued by the Secretary of State confirming that a business is legally registered, active, and compliant with state requirements. It shows that the company has filed all necessary reports, paid required fees, and is authorized to do business in that state. We offer this service in all states. To view more information about Certificate of Good Standing, please refer to the Knowledge Base

Most customers don't need a COGS unless a third party requests it, such as when:

  • Opening a business bank account
  • Applying for financing or credit
  • Registering to do business in another state (foreign qualification)
  • Obtaining a business license or government contract
  • Proving legitimacy to vendors or partners

How to Obtain

A COGS is not automatically included in the formation process; customers typically purchase it through us when a bank, government agency, or other third party requests it. We retrieve the certificate directly from the state and deliver it to the customer's dashboard, typically within a few business days.

Requirements

To receive a COGS, businesses must comply with the following requirements:

  • Active & In Good Standing — Verified through the state's public records at the time the certificate is requested.
  • No Overdue Reports or Fees — Any outstanding balance or missed filing will cause the state to deny the request until resolved.
  • Registered Agent on File — If the agent has resigned or is no longer valid, the certificate cannot be issued until a replacement is appointed.

If these conditions aren't met, the state will refuse to issue the certificate. Any previous lack of compliance will not be reflected. Once the certificate is issued, it confirms that the company is currently in full compliance and in good standing with the state.

Important Considerations
1
Expiration

A COGS is only valid at the moment it is issued. Most third parties require one dated within 30–90 days, so customers may need to request a new one if too much time has passed.

2
State Turnaround

Processing times vary by state. Some states issue the certificate within a day; others may take several business days. Rush processing is not always available.

3
Not a Tax Document

A COGS confirms compliance with state registration requirements only. It is not related to tax filings or IRS status and cannot be used in place of a tax clearance certificate.

4
One State at a Time

A COGS is issued per state. If a business is registered in multiple states, a separate certificate must be obtained from each state where good standing needs to be verified.
