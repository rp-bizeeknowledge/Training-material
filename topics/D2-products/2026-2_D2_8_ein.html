Employer Identification Number (EIN)

Business Tax Identification Number required for tax filing, banking and payroll purposes
What It Is

An Employer Identification Number (EIN), also called a Federal Tax Identification Number or Tax ID, is a unique nine-digit number issued by the IRS to identify a business entity for tax and reporting purposes.

While these are the entities we can obtain it for, an EIN is also required for partnerships (general or limited), multiple-member LLCs, LLCs taxed as corporations, and single-member LLCs that hire employees.

For sole proprietorships, an EIN is only required if they have employees or establish certain retirement plans, it's otherwise common that the owners use their own SSN. Many still choose to get one to separate finances, build credit, or establish business legitimacy.

Required For These Activities
1
Tax Returns
The EIN identifies the business when filing federal and state tax returns. It replaces the owner's SSN on all business tax documents submitted to the IRS.
2
Hiring Employees
Any business that hires employees must have an EIN to withhold federal income taxes, report payroll taxes, and issue W-2 forms at year end.
3
Business Banking
Banks require an EIN to open a dedicated business account, keeping business finances legally separate from personal assets.
4
Licenses & Credit
Many states require an EIN when applying for business licenses or permits. Lenders and vendors also use it to establish and verify business credit.

An EIN is not public information. It is not published in public databases and is used by the IRS and authorized parties (banks, government agencies) to identify the business for tax and financial purposes.

Obtainment of an EIN

According to IRS guidelines, the EIN should be obtained after the business entity has been officially formed. This prevents name mismatches, which could cause delays.

To apply for an EIN, IRS Form SS-4 has to be submitted either online or by fax. There is no fee when applying directly through the IRS. However, third-party services may charge for filing on the customer's behalf.

As part of the application, a responsible party must be assigned. This is the person who ultimately owns or controls the business and its assets. In most cases, this is the owner, majority shareholder, or managing member, as they are directly involved in the company's financial and tax matters and have a vested interest in its compliance.

Although the IRS does technically allow any individual to be named the responsible party, even someone not affiliated with the company, this is uncommon and not recommended. The responsible party is expected to be available to respond to IRS inquiries and represent the business if needed. Listing someone without real authority can result in complications or delays.

  5. Go to the EIN tab in DB, click on edit, and paste the number in the EIN box on the right.

When to Obtain a New EIN

A new EIN is required if the business undergoes major structural changes, such as forming a new legal entity, merging with another company, or being acquired. However, a simple business name change does not require a new EIN as long as the entity structure remains the same.
