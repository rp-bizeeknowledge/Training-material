Employer Identification

Who needs it · Obtainment · Forms & documents · Updating records
What It Is

An Employer Identification Number (EIN), also called a Federal Tax Identification Number or Tax ID, is a unique nine-digit number issued by the IRS to identify a business entity for tax and reporting purposes.

Who Needs an EIN

The IRS requires most business entities to obtain an EIN. This includes:

Partnerships (general or limited)
Corporations (C Corps and S Corps)
Nonprofit organizations
Multiple-member LLCs
Single-member LLCs, if they have employees or elect to be taxed as a corporation
LLCs taxed as corporations (C or S)
Sole proprietorships, only if they have employees or establish certain retirement plans

For sole proprietorships without employees or retirement plans, an EIN is generally not required; they can use the owner's Social Security Number (SSN). However, many choose to get an EIN to separate personal and business finances, build credit, or establish business legitimacy.

It is required for activities such as:

Filing federal and state tax returns
Hiring employees
Opening a business bank account
Applying for business licenses or credit
Privacy Note

An EIN is not public information. It is not published in public databases and is used by the IRS and authorized parties (banks, government agencies) to identify the business for tax and financial purposes.

Obtainment of an EIN

According to IRS guidelines, the EIN should be obtained after the business entity has been officially formed. This prevents name mismatches, which would potentially generate delays.

To apply for an EIN, IRS Form SS-4 has to be submitted, either online or by fax. There is no fee when applying directly through the IRS. However, third-party services may charge for filing on the customer's behalf.

As part of the application, a responsible party must be assigned. This is the person who ultimately owns or controls the business and its assets. In most cases, this is the owner, majority shareholder, or managing member, as they are directly involved in the company's financial and tax matters and have a vested interest in its compliance.

Although the IRS does technically allow any individual to be named the responsible party, even someone not affiliated with the company, this is uncommon and not recommended. The responsible party is expected to be available to respond to IRS inquiries and represent the business if needed. Listing someone without real authority can result in complications or delays.

Updating Our Records

When a customer reaches out asking how to add their EIN information to the dashboard when we did not receive the document on their behalf, use the following steps:

Option A: Faxed application that did NOT use our VA

  1. Confirm that the order is Shipped and it has a signed SS-4 in the Internal Documents tab on DB (Main Orders only).
  2. Send an email request for proof of EIN (CP 575 or 147C) in PDF format.
  3. Check that the company name on the form matches the one on file.
  4. Upload the PDF file to the Document Upload Tab in DB. This will allow customers to see the form on their completed documents moving forward.

Option B: Obtained without using our services

  1. Confirm that the order is Shipped and there are no linked EIN services.
  2. Request for proof of EIN (CP 575 or 147C) in PDF.
  3. Check that the company name on the form matches the one on file.
  4. Copy the EIN from the document provided.
  5. Go to the EIN tab in DB, click on edit, and paste the number in the EIN box on the right.

EIN Forms & Documents

Form SS-4: Application for EIN

Form SS-4 is the application used to request an Employer Identification Number from the IRS. We use this form whenever applying for an EIN on behalf of foreign customers, customers who refused to provide an SSN, or as an alternative for domestic (electronic) applications that were rejected by the IRS. This form does not create a business on its own. It is simply the document the IRS uses to assign the company's federal tax ID number.

Form 8821: Tax Authorization

IRS Form 8821, "Tax Information Authorization", lets an individual or entity access taxpayer information. This enables us to contact the IRS on the client's behalf and access their EIN details for updates or retrieval. At Bizee, we submit this form along with the SS-4 application to access and review the clients' EIN information as a third party before it is issued. We only access EIN-related information, so clients do not need to worry about other data being shared. Clients must sign this form on their dashboard after signing the SS-4. They cannot sign Form 8821 before the SS-4. *Note: This authorization expires once the IRS issues the EIN. Any corrections or issues with the 8821 should be escalated to the EIN/SS-4 department.

CP 575: EIN Confirmation Letter

The CP 575 letter is sent by the IRS, either by fax or mail, to confirm that an EIN has been assigned. It is the original EIN confirmation notice, issued right after the SS-4 (faxed or electronic) is processed and approved. CP 575 Letters can have several different versions, some of which contain a dotted slip customers can mail to the IRS to request changes, while other taxpayers are required to send a signed letter.

EIN Verification Letter (147C)

An IRS Letter 147C is an official document issued by the IRS to verify an existing Employer Identification Number. It serves as a replacement for the original CP 575 EIN confirmation notice, which is issued only once and cannot be reissued if lost. Businesses typically request a 147C letter when they need official, documented proof of their EIN for banks, vendors, or other institutions. In some cases, customers can request a 147C letter from the IRS directly and use our fax number if we filed for their EIN, as it is already on the IRS's records.

When to Obtain a New EIN

A new EIN is required if the business undergoes major structural changes, such as forming a new legal entity, merging with another company, or being acquired. However, a simple business name change does not require a new EIN as long as the entity structure remains the same.
