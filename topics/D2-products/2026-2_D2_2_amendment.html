Amendment

    Official state filing used to formally change a company's permanent legal structure, such as its business name, corporate purpose, or ownership structure
    What It Is

    An Amendment is a filing used to update a company's official information with the state. It's typically needed when making changes to the business name, address, management, or purpose. Once filed, the updated information becomes part of the public record.

    When Is It Needed

    Not all business details can be updated through an Amendment. Some information, such as a company's mailing address or shareholder details, is not recorded by the state and should be updated internally in documents like the Operating Agreement or Corporate Bylaws.

    An Amendment is only required when the information being changed appears in the state's public records, such as the business name, the number of authorized shares, the names of members, directors or officers, or the business activity/NAICS codes depending on the state.

    To know whether an Amendment is needed, customers should review their Articles of Incorporation or Organization and compare them with the information they want to change. The state's filing instructions or resource guide can also help confirm what must be amended.

    Important Considerations
    • Occasionally, shares are presented with a par value, which may require an amendment for any change.
    • If shareholder information is not listed with the state or has already been amended, updates can be made upon request via email from the customer.
    • An amendment allows for multiple changes to be made at once. You can change the company name, add or remove a member, and update the address all in a single amendment. It is not limited to one change per amendment.

    Shareholder details are never disclosed in any state's public records.

    Updating an EIN

    Sometimes clients can think that an amendment will help them change their information with the IRS. However, to make changes to a company's EIN records, such as the name or address, customers should contact the IRS directly using either:

    Sending a signed letter that that contains the following details:

    • Legal business name
    • Employer Identification Number (EIN)
    • Current business address on file with the IRS
    • Full name and title or role of the responsible party
    • Daytime phone number and best time to reach you
    • A clear description of each requested change (i.e., business name, address, or authorized individual)


    The slip at the bottom of their CP575 Confirmation Letter (if available)

    Bottom Slip · CP575 Confirmation Letter

    Bizee cannot update this information on a taxpayer's behalf through an amendment. Changes to an existing EIN need to be done by the responsible party exclusively and might also require the submission of Form 8821-B.

    Delaware

    Delaware allows customers to decide whether or not to list company members. If a customer initially chose not to list members or the company address, they can file an amendment later to add this information.

    Pennsylvania

    Pennsylvania doesn't show members on the Articles until the first Annual Report has been filed. If customers want to change this information, they will have to wait until the report's deadline is near so the change can be made through an Amendment.

    Updating DBA Address After Company Address Amendment

    When a customer files an amendment to change the company address with the Secretary of State, the DBA (Doing Business As) address does not automatically update. The customer will need to update the DBA address directly with the county where the DBA was initially filed.
