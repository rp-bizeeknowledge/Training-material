What It Is

An Annual Report is a periodic filing that helps keep a business in good standing with the state. It is typically used to confirm that the business is still active and that key information on file remains current.

An Annual Report is not the same as a tax return. Its main purpose is to keep the state's business records up to date. Depending on the state, the filing may confirm information such as the business address, Registered Agent, and in some cases, the names of its owners, members, directors, or managers.

Some states may also request limited business information, such as estimated revenue ranges. Even when financial details are included, the Annual Report remains separate from filing income taxes with the IRS or a specialized state agency.

Most Annual Reports require payment of a state filing fee. Missing the deadline may lead to additional penalties, such as late fees, loss of good standing, or even administrative dissolution, depending on the state's rules.

In simple terms, an Annual Report tells the state that the business is still operating and that its information is current.

Late Fee Policy

Some states apply a late fee when a filing is submitted after the state's deadline. To help avoid delays and ensure orders can be processed in a timely manner, any applicable late fee is automatically added at checkout when the filing date is close to the state deadline.

The fee cannot be removed at checkout if the customer is placing the order less than 3 days before the deadline, but can be refunded if ultimately not needed.

No Changes Allowed via Annual Report

In some states, customers may be able to update business information through the same filing used for Annual Reports. For example, California uses the Statement of Information for both compliance filings and certain business updates. However, Bizee processes business changes through Amendment services and reserves Annual Reports for compliance purposes only. This approach keeps our filing process consistent across states and entity types.

Company information (i.e., business name, address, members, etc.) cannot be changed through an annual report. These must be handled through a separate Amendment filing.

Reminders

We send email or phone reminders to help customers stay compliant with Annual Reports. These are courtesy notices to help avoid state-imposed penalties.
