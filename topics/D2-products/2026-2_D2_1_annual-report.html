Annual Report

What It Is

An annual report is a required filing that helps keep a business in good standing with the state. It is not a requirement in all states. Sometimes it is required per entity type, meaning that in a certain state like New Mexico, LLCs are exempt from Annual Report filings while C-Corps are due biennially and Non-profits are due annually.

For first-time entrepreneurs, it's important to know that an annual report is not the same as a tax return. In many states, the report is simply a compliance filing. It acts as a notice to the state that your business is still active and confirms basic information like your address, registered agent, and owners or managers.

In some states, the annual report may also ask for limited financial or tax-related information, such as estimated revenue ranges. Even then, it is still separate from filing income taxes with the IRS or the state.

Annual reports are usually due once a year and often include a filing fee. If the report is not filed on time, the state may charge late fees, mark the business as not in good standing, or dissolve the business.

In simple terms, an annual report tells the state, "This business still exists, and here is its current information." Filing it on time is an important part of keeping a business entity legally active.

Late Fee Policy

Some states apply a late fee when a filing is submitted after the state's deadline. To help avoid delays and ensure orders can be processed in a timely manner, any applicable late fee is automatically added at checkout when the filing date is close to the state deadline.

Refundable late fee · Annual Report checkout

The fee cannot be removed at checkout if the customer is placing the order less than 3 days before the deadline, but can be refunded if ultimately not needed.

No Changes Allowed via Annual Report

Company information (i.e., business name, address, members, etc.) cannot be changed through an annual report. These must be handled through a separate Amendment filing.

As a third party, we can't make those changes through the annual report. It can be done, just not through us. We apologize for the inconvenience.

It can happen that the client is familiar with how the state works, for example, California. Amendments and Annual Reports are both the Statement of Information. To streamline our process, we make changes through amendments and use the annual report for compliance matters regardless of whether the state allows changes to be done through the same type of filing.

Reminders

We send email or phone reminders to help customers stay compliant with Annual Reports. These are courtesy notices to help avoid state-imposed penalties.
