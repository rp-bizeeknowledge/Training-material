Assumed Business Name (DBA)

File for a company that needs to do business under a fictitious name
What It Is

A DBA is a business nickname. It lets you operate under a different name than your official legal name. Useful for companies looking to rebrand for a specific product or service, or for sole proprietors who don't want to use their real name or create a company. Important Considerations

This is not a service we offer in every state. When we do offer it, we typically file the DBA with the Secretary of State, but that's not always the case; some states require it to be filed at the county level instead.

If the DBA is filed with the state, the document will generally reflect that the name is valid across all counties within that state.

However, if the DBA is filed at the county level, it will only be valid in that specific county. If the customer wants the DBA to be recognized in multiple counties, we would need to file it separately in each county.

Conversions

When a customer says they want to "convert" their DBA to an LLC or Corporation, what they're likely referring to is changing the structure of their business entity from a sole proprietorship (or a partnership) to a formal LLC or Corporation. But here's the important part:

We do not support conversions, which means we don't change one type of entity to another, like converting a sole proprietorship or a DBA into an LLC or Corporation. Instead, we form new companies. So, if a customer wants to turn their sole proprietorship into an LLC or Corporation, they'll need to form a new LLC or Corporation and then update their business documents, such as their DBA registration, if they want to keep using the same trade name.

Make sure to explain this clearly to the customer, and let them know that forming a new LLC or Corporation is a separate process from updating their DBA. If they insist on being advised on which option to choose or how to proceed with a conversion, refer them to a business advisor.
