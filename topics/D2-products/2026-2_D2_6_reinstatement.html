Reinstatement

Required filing when you are bringing your business back to 'Good Standing' with the state
What It Is

A Reinstatement is the process of restoring an inactive, non-compliant, or administratively dissolved company back into good standing with the State of formation, as if the company had never been deregistered.

This is typically needed when a business has missed compulsory filings, such as the Annual Report, or failed to maintain a Registered Agent, causing the state to suspend or administratively dissolve the entity.

Additional Funds Requests
Additional Funds Request · Example

Placing a Reinstatement order requires payment of both the applicable state fee and Bizee's processing fee. After the order is submitted, the Filing Department will contact the state to confirm whether any additional amounts are owed, such as overdue Annual Report fees or state-assessed penalties needed to return the company to good standing.

If additional funds are required, an order issue will be generated, and payment must be submitted before the filing can move forward.

Important Notes
  • Reinstatements for Voluntarily-Dissolved Companies: Not all states allow a company that was formally dissolved by its owners to be reinstated, and some states require a complex process to do so. Because of this, we offer this in a limited number of states. Always check the Knowledge Base to confirm whether the service is available in the customer's state.
  • Reinstatements for Involuntarily-Dissolved Companies: When a customer wants to formally dissolve their company, and this has been dissolved by the state, they first have to reinstate the entity. After the company is current with all overdue payments and back in good standing, they can file for Articles of Dissolution.
  • Foreign Qualifications: We do not handle reinstatement filings for foreign qualifications (i.e., businesses registered in states other than their formation state).
