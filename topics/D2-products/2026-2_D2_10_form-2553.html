Form 2553 (S-Corp Election)

Submission · IRS response · Eligibility · Corrections · Late elections
What It Is

IRS Form 2553 (Election by a Small Business Corporation), is used to elect S Corporation tax status for federal tax purposes. Eligible domestic corporations and limited liability companies that meet IRS requirements may file this form to be taxed as an S Corporation rather than under the default tax classification.

By making this election, the business generally becomes a pass-through entity for federal income tax purposes, meaning income, losses, deductions, and credits are reported on the owners' individual tax returns rather than being taxed at the corporate level. The election affects how federal income and employment taxes are calculated and reported but does not change the entity's legal structure under state law.

Submission
📤

Once Form 2553 is faxed to the IRS, the service is complete. The faxed document will be available for customers to view.

IRS Response
📬

The IRS will send an acceptance or rejection letter, usually within 60 days. The letter is sent to the address on the form. Bizee won't know the outcome unless the customer uses our Virtual Address.

Eligibility
🚫

Foreigners cannot apply for S-Corp status.

ℹ️

The business must have an EIN and signatures from all owners.

Common Customer Scenarios
Form Rejection: Ask for the rejection letter and escalate.
Missing Information: The system auto-fills missing fields (like EIN).
Form Not Available: If the form option is missing, the company may not be eligible for S-Corp status.
Corrections
✓

Corrections can be made before the fax is sent.

✗

After faxing, corrections can only be made if the IRS rejects the form. The customer must contact the IRS directly.

Election Requirements
ℹ️

Form 2553 must be submitted after obtaining an EIN.

ℹ️

Must be signed by all owners.

🚫

The company cannot have other entities as members.

Effective Date
📅

The form must be submitted before the effective date (within 75 days). The effective date can be postdated to a future time. It determines when S-Corp status takes effect.

Late Election
⏰

A Late Election can be filed up to 3 years and 75 days after the deadline, but requires approval from the Document Department.
