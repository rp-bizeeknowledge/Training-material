Foreign Qualification

Official filing used to branch out as a foreign entity into new states
What It Is

A Foreign Qualification is the legal process that allows an existing company to operate in a state other than where it was originally formed. It ensures the business is authorized to operate in both its home state and the new state where it wishes to conduct business. Foreign qualified entities will need to comply with both states' regulations and meet annual reporting and tax obligations. To view more information about Foreign Qualification, please refer to the Knowledge Base.

Special Scenarios

Desired Name Not Available in the Foreign State

Issue: The desired business name is not available in the foreign-qualified state.

Solution: The company will need to file for a DBA (Doing Business As) or Assumed Business Name in the foreign state. The company will still operate under its original name, but will use a different name in that state.

Action: Gather additional information for the DBA filing in the foreign state.

Dissolution or Reinstatement of Foreign Qualification (Withdrawal)

Issue: The desired business name is not available in the foreign-qualified state.

Solution: The company will need to file for a DBA (Doing Business As) or Assumed Business Name in the foreign state. The company will still operate under its original name, but will use a different name in that state.

Action: Gather additional information for the DBA filing in the foreign state.
