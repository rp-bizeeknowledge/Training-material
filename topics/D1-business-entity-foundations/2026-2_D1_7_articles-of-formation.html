3 — Articles of Formation (Filed Articles)

When a customer purchases a formation package from Bizee, it creates what we call a Main Order. This Main Order triggers the filing process with the state and results in the creation of the Filed Articles, the official document that confirms the creation of the business entity. Think of it as the birth certificate of a company. Filed Articles can only be generated through a Main Order. They are not available for standalone purchase.

Filed Articles are the state-issued documents that confirm a business has been legally formed:

🏢

LLCs

Articles of Organization

🏛️

Corporations / Nonprofits

Articles of Incorporation

3.1 — Delivery of Filed Articles

By default, customers receive digital PDF copies of the Filed Articles, accessible from their online dashboard. These copies are printable and carry the same legal weight as physical documents. Customers sometimes have the option of paying an additional $25 at checkout to receive a physical copy of the Filed Articles mailed to the contact address provided. When purchased, they are sent within 3 to 5 business days after the company is officially formed.

3.2 — Updating Filed Articles

If a customer later needs to change the information listed on the filed articles, such as the business name, company address, or management structure, these changes cannot be made by editing or reissuing the original Filed Articles. Instead, the customer must file a separate document called an Amendment.

Think of it like buying a used car:

🚗 The Used Car Analogy

Filed Articles
= The original title of ownership: it's proof of the initial setup.
Amendment
= The title transfer: it doesn't erase the original sale but updates the record to show who owns it now or what's changed.

An Amendment does not replace the Filed Articles. It simply becomes an additional record in the state's system, confirming that a change has occurred. The original filing always remains on record with the state for historical and legal reference.

3.3 — Examples
3.3.1 — Limited Liability Company (LLC)

If the customer ordered an LLC, what they receive in return are Articles of Organization.

3.3.2 — C Corp, S Corp & NFP

If one of the above entity types is chosen instead, customers receive Articles of Incorporation, which may also be called a Certificate of Formation or Incorporation, depending on the state.

4 — The Role of the Organizer

The role of the Organizer/Incorporator is limited to filing the necessary forms with the state and overseeing the formation process. Organizers are not owners or ongoing decision-makers. Once the company is formed, control automatically shifts to the actual members (LLCs) or directors (Corporations).

When a company is formed through our services, we include a Statement of the Organizer (for LLCs) or Statement of the Incorporator (for Corporations and Nonprofits). These confirm who filed the paperwork and show they stepped down after formation.

📌

If ownership needs to be proven, the Statement, along with the Operating Agreement or corporate bylaws, can be used. This is especially helpful in states that do not list members or directors in the Articles of Organization or Incorporation.
