The Lifecycle of a Main Order

Review · Hold · Processing · Forwarded · Shipped · Cancelations & Refunds
Overview

We aim to make the business formation process smooth and transparent. To support that goal, every order placed through our platform follows a clearly defined lifecycle, from the moment a customer submits their application to the completion of the formation process.

This guide provides a detailed overview of each stage an order goes through, including how status changes occur, what triggers holds, how refunds and reactivations work, and what actions are appropriate based on department responsibilities. Understanding this process helps ensure accurate handling of customer inquiries and internal workflows.

About this status

This is the initial stage for every Main Order. Customers can make limited edits directly through the dashboard, until midnight of the order day (12:00 AM CST). While removing entire sections is not allowed, they may correct specific fields in case of mistakes or second thoughts.

If changes are needed that cannot be made through the dashboard, the order must be canceled and refunded, and a new order must be placed.

About this status

The Filing Department is actively handling the application. Key activities include:

  • Verifying company name availability
  • Ensuring all fields are correctly completed
  • Checking for errors or inconsistencies
  • Preparing and compiling documents for state submission

If issues arise requiring customer input, the order may temporarily move to Hold. Once all requirements are met, the order progresses to Forwarded.

Forwarded
Highlights

Click an icon to see more details.

About this status

The application has been submitted to the state for review and approval. No changes can be made at this point.

If the state rejects the application, it's usually due to:

  • Company name issues
  • Other state-specific filing requirements

If rejected, the order returns to Processing for resolution. If accepted, it proceeds to Shipped.

Shipped
Highlights

About this status

Formation is complete. The completed documents are made available in the customer's dashboard for download and viewing, and a completion email is sent notifying them.

This is the final stage of the lifecycle; no further changes or actions are required.

The only changes that can be made to an order regardless of the status are NAICS code updates (in states that don't list this information) or the ownership percentages (for LLCs).

Hold

An order can be placed on hold by various departments for the following reasons:

Customer-Initiated Hold

Customers may request a hold through their dashboard while the order is on Review Status, or contact Support to request it while under Processing Status as well (for example, if they need more time to decide).

Allowed Changes
  • Company name
  • Members name/address
  • Company address
  • Mailing address
  • Add Expedited Processing (if shown in dashboard)
  • Contact information
  • Billing information
  • Add or remove RA subscription
  • EIN information
  • NAICS Codes
Restricted Changes
  • Changing entity types
  • Changing the formation state
  • Adding or removing
    members/officers/directors
  • Upgrading or downgrading package tiers

These holds last for up to 3 months. If no action is taken within that timeframe, the order will be automatically canceled without a refund.

Filing Department Hold

After moving to "Processing," the filing department reviews the application for accuracy and compliance with state requirements. A hold may be placed for:

  • Company Name Issues or unavailability
  • Spelling Confirmation needs
  • Incomplete or Invalid addresses
  • Missing or unclear business purpose

In such cases, the filing team will email the customer requesting additional information. There is no limit to the number of name change attempts until the state approves one.

These holds last for up to 3 months. If no action is taken within that timeframe, the order will be automatically canceled without a refund.

Billing Department Hold

If an order is flagged for potential fraud, the billing department will place it on hold so the customer can complete an Identity Confirmation process, where the following must be provided:

  • A photo of their government-issued ID
  • A selfie
  • Card-related information

Once the documentation is reviewed and approved, the hold should be released within 24 to 48 hours.

This hold lasts up to 5 business days. If the required documentation is not submitted in time, the order will be canceled and refunded.

Customer Service Hold

Customer Service Agents may place an order on hold upon request (for example, if the customer needs time to review their options).

When the customer requests that the order be resumed, Agents should only move the order to "Processing", not Review. The "Review" status does not trigger any internal action and will cause delays.

These holds last for up to 3 months. If no action is taken within that timeframe, the order will be automatically canceled without a refund.

Payment Rejection Holds

If a payment fails, the system will automatically place the order on hold and send the customer an email prompting them to complete the payment.

These holds last for up to 3 months. If no action is taken within that timeframe, the order will be automatically canceled without a refund.

Cancelations, Refunds & Reactivations
Orders that were canceled but not refunded may be reinstated at any moment upon customer request.
Refunds are available as long as the order has not been submitted to the state and no state fees have been paid.
If services have already been partially rendered, a partial refund may be issued based on the work completed.

No Changes Allowed

Once an order reaches Forwarded status, the filing has been submitted to the Secretary of State exactly as entered. At this point:

  • The information is in the state's hands and the process cannot be stopped or recalled.
  • Our internal policy does not allow us to request changes to a filing that is already under state review.
  • The order must be completed as submitted. Any corrections will require a separate Amendment filing after the formation is approved.
  • If the state rejects the application, the order returns to Processing and the Filing Department will address the issue at that point.

Order Completion Email Is Sent

Once the formation is complete and the documents are uploaded to the dashboard, a completion email is automatically sent to the customer notifying them that their company is officially formed. The email includes:

A message confirming the company has been filed
A direct link to access the filed documents in their dashboard
A Bank of America or Relay Business Banking Offer
