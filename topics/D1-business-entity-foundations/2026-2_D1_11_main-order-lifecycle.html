The Lifecycle of a Main Order

Review · Hold · Processing · Forwarded · Shipped · Cancellations & Refunds
Overview

We aim to make the business formation process smooth and transparent. To support that goal, every order placed through our platform follows a clearly defined lifecycle, from the moment a customer submits their application to the completion of the formation process.

This guide provides a detailed overview of each stage an order goes through, including how status changes occur, what triggers holds, how refunds and reactivations work, and what actions are appropriate based on department responsibilities. Understanding this process helps ensure accurate handling of customer inquiries and internal workflows.

Status Details
1

Review Status

Once an order is placed, it begins in the "Review" status.

During this stage, customers may make limited edits until midnight of the same day (12:00am CST) the order was submitted. While customers cannot add or remove entire sections, they may correct specific fields in case of mistakes or second thoughts.

If changes are needed that cannot be made through the dashboard, the order must be canceled and refunded, and a new order must be placed.

2

Processing

Once an order moves into "Processing," it is actively being handled by the Filing Department. This status means the team is reviewing all provided information for accuracy, completeness, and compliance with state-specific requirements.

Key activities during the Processing stage include:

  • Verifying company name availability
  • Ensuring all fields are correctly completed
  • Checking for errors or inconsistencies
  • Preparing and compiling documents for state submission

If issues arise that require customer input, the order may temporarily move to Hold. Once all requirements are met, the order progresses to Forwarded.

3

Forwarded

When an order reaches the "Forwarded" status, it means it's been submitted to the state for review and approval. At this stage, no changes can be made.

If the state rejects the application, it's usually due to:

  • Company name issues
  • Other state-specific filing requirements

If the application is accepted, the order proceeds to the final status.

4

Shipped

"Shipped" indicates that the company formation is complete, and all relevant documents have been finalized.

This is the final stage of the process. The completed documents are made available in the customer's dashboard for download and viewing.

Hold

An order can be placed on hold by various departments for the following reasons:

👤 Customer-Initiated Hold

Customers may request a hold through their dashboard while the order is on Review Status, or contact Support to request it while under Processing Status as well (i.e., if they need more time to decide).

These holds last for up to 3 months. If no action is taken within that timeframe, the order will be automatically canceled without a refund.

📁 Filing Department Hold

After moving to "Processing," the filing department reviews the application for accuracy and compliance with state requirements. A hold may be placed for:

  • Company Name Issues or unavailability
  • Spelling Confirmation needs
  • Incomplete or Invalid addresses
  • Missing or unclear business purpose

In such cases, the filing team will email the customer requesting additional information. There is no limit to the number of name change attempts until the state approves one.

💳 Billing Department Hold

If an order is flagged for potential fraud, the billing department will place it on hold for up to 5 business days so the customer can complete an Identity Confirmation process, where the following must be provided:

  • A photo of their government-issued ID
  • A selfie
  • Card-related information

If the requested documents are not submitted in time, the order will be canceled and refunded. Once the documentation is reviewed and approved, the hold should be released within 24 to 48 hours.

🎧 Customer Service Hold

Customer Service Agents may place an order on hold upon request (i.e., if the customer needs time to review their options).

When the customer requests that the order be resumed, Agents should only move the order to "Processing", not Review. The "Review" status does not trigger any internal action and will cause delays.

💳 Payment Rejection Holds

If a payment fails, the system will automatically place the order on hold and send the customer an email prompting them to complete the payment.

Cancellations, Refunds & Reactivations
Orders that were canceled but not refunded may be reinstated at any moment upon customer request.
Refunds are available as long as the order has not been submitted to the state and no state fees have been paid.
If services have already been partially rendered, a partial refund may be issued based on the work completed.

Note: If an issue falls outside the scope of customer service (e.g., filing corrections or billing verification), agents should not attempt to resolve it directly. Instead, customers must be instructed to follow the steps outlined in the email sent by the appropriate department.

The only changes that can be made to an order regardless of the status are NAICS code updates (in states that don't list this information) or the ownership percentages (for LLCs).
