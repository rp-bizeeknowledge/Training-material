Zendesk Support

A guide to the zendesk ecosystem and ticket lifecycle
What It Is

At Bizee, Zendesk is used as the centralized system to manage all customer interactions, including phone calls and emails. Each time an existing or prospective customer contacts us through any of these channels, a Support Ticket is automatically created and assigned a unique ticket number for tracking and reference purposes.

Glossary
Ticket
A single record in Zendesk that represents a customer request, question, or issue, including all related messages, updates, and actions taken to resolve it.
User
Any person registered in Zendesk who interacts with the system (End User/Staff Member).
Assignee
The agent or group currently responsible for handling and resolving a ticket.
Requester
The user who originally submitted the ticket. This is typically the customer requesting assistance.
CC (Carbon Copy)
A feature that allows additional users to be included on a ticket so they can receive public replies and updates.
Group
A collection of agents organized by role, expertise, or function, used to route and manage tickets efficiently.
Escalation Box
A designated field used to flag and forward cases that require special handling, input, or review by another team.
Internal Note
A private note visible only to staff, used to document internal context, actions taken, or instructions that should not be shared with the customer.
Public Reply
A response sent through the ticket that is visible to the customer and any users included in CC.

Example: The agent sends a public reply explaining the next steps in the process.
CSAT
Customer Satisfaction, a metric used to measure customer satisfaction based on a survey sent after a ticket is resolved.
Ticket Lifecycle

Tickets are the core of a Support Agent's daily work. The diagram below describes the different statuses a ticket can move through from the moment it is created and until it's inactive.

New

The ticket is received but not yet picked up by an agent. This status is defined by the system.

Open

The ticket needs follow-up and has not been escalated yet.

Pending

The ticket has been escalated and is awaiting response.

On-Hold

Ticket is paused, waiting for supervisor action. For supervisors only.

Solved

The issue has been resolved; no further action is required.

Closed

The system automatically closes tickets that spend a defined period of time solved.

Zendesk Merging Guidelines
Tickets

✅ DO

  • Merge tickets when multiple tickets exist for the same issue. Consolidate them into the ticket you will actively respond to, ensuring all customer questions are addressed in a single thread.
  • Merge new tickets into your existing open or pending ticket when they relate to the same request. This helps prevent duplicate work and avoids unnecessary or duplicate escalations.

❌ DON'T

  • Merge tickets into solved tickets, unless the ticket documents a callback or action that occurred before the issue was resolved and needs to be referenced.
  • Merge a ticket into another ticket that has not been reviewed or worked on.
How To Merge: Tickets

Option A: Merging One Ticket into Yours

1

Click on the 3 dots in the top right corner. Once you see the drop-down menu, click the option "Merge into another ticket".

2

A window will prompt you to enter a ticket number. This number identifies the ticket that the current one will be merged into. Confirm the number is correct, then select Merge.

3

Make sure to deselect the checkboxes that say "User can see this comment" (if applicable).

Be careful: once a ticket is merged, no one can unmerge it.

Option B: Merging Various Tickets from Your Views

1

Select the tickets that will be merged into another ticket.

2

Click on "merge" at the bottom of the screen.

3

Type in the ticket number you'll be merging into or select it from the list underneath the box, and click merge.

Option C: Merging Various Tickets from the Same User

1

Click on the tab showing the name of the user in the upper left-hand corner and select the tickets you will be merging. (Do not select the ticket that the others will be merged into).

B

Click on the down arrow on the black box in the upper right-hand corner and select "Merge tickets into another ticket...".

C

Select the ticket to be merged into, located underneath the box, or type in the ticket number in the box, and click "Merge".

Users

✅ DO

  • Merge users when a customer already has an account, but the email address or phone number they are using is not linked to their existing orders.
  • Authenticate the customer before merging any user records. Identity verification must always be completed first.
  • Offer to merge the information proactively to keep the customer's account consolidated and avoid future confusion.
  • Obtain clear and explicit customer consent before proceeding with the merge, regardless of whether the interaction is by phone or email.
  • Use clear consent language, such as: "Would you like me to link this phone number (or email) to your account so we can keep everything in one place moving forward?"

❌ DON'T

  • Merge users without customer consent, even if the information appears to belong to the same person.
  • Merge users before authentication has been completed.
  • Assume consent based on context or prior interactions.
  • Merge user records unnecessarily if the contact information is already correctly linked.
How To Merge: Users
1

Click on the customer name.

2

Click on the down arrow located in the upper-right-hand corner of the screen to see the drop-down menu and select the option "Merge into another user".

3

Enter the email address or the customer's name and click merge.

Annotation Guidelines

Always add clear, concise internal notes to every ticket you modify. Include the following:

  • Order number (if applicable)
  • Relevant interaction details
  • Actions taken
  • Next steps (if applicable)
