D7 · Section 04

Interaction Flow

Greeting · Query · Authentication · Support · Closing

Stay real, stay ready. No scripts.

Greeting

Start the call by introducing yourself and the company. Then, warmly ask how you can assist the customer.

Query

Figure out what the customer needs by listening actively and asking probing questions. And authenticate only if needed.

Authentication

We protect customer data by verifying account holders before sharing or changing account information. Authenticate when you cannot pull up the account using Zendesk user details, and the customer wants to:

Access account-specific information
Make changes
Cancel a subscription

Based on information sensitivity, some questions require authentication and some do not:

No Authentication Required

  • "How long does it take to file an LLC?"
  • "How often are Annual Reports required in Oklahoma?"
  • "I need to start a new Corporation"

Authentication Required

  • "Can I get my EIN?"
  • "Please cancel my service."
  • "I need to update my company's membership"
How to Authenticate

Step 1: Check the Database: Review the customer's billing information to determine what to ask. If no payment method is on file, adjust your questions.

Step 2: Request for authentication information

If a Credit or Debit Card is on File

A

Name + Last 4 digits of card, or full billing address

Ask for the customer's name (must match the account) plus the last 4 digits of the card, or full billing address.

B

Last 4 digits of the SSN

If they cannot provide either from A: ask for the last 4 digits of the SSN (if EIN is included in the order).

C

Call or email from contact info on file

If still unavailable: ask them to call or email us from the contact information on file.

D

Photo ID via email

As a last resort: send an email to the preferred address, asking for a photo of their ID.

Important: Apple Pay uses a temporary card number. The last 4 digits in the database will not match the customer's real card, so do not use them for authentication.

Step 3: If the Caller Is Not a Verified Contact

📋

Ask to speak directly with the verified contact or offer to transfer the call to them.

🚫

If they cannot authenticate, do not provide account-specific help.

Roles and Permissions
Contact
Can receive information and request changes.
Client
Can receive information but can only make changes through their dashboard.
Owner (green check in DB)
Can receive information but can only make changes through their dashboard.
Special Scenarios
If a verified contact is present with the caller, authenticate the contact directly.
If the sole member of a company managed by a commercial client wants ownership: send an email requesting their ID, collect information to add them as a user in the database, and escalate using Zendesk (by selecting your Supervisor in the dropdown) and mark the ticket as pending.

Key rule: Do not tell customers to "reach out from the email or phone on file." Always follow the full authentication process.

Support

After the customer expresses their concern, and has been authenticated, address the issue clearly and helpfully. Before ending the call, offer any additional support or information they may need.

I'm glad I was able to assist. Is there anything else I can help with?

Closing

End with a friendly goodbye and then document the call, document any actions taken and call details in an internal note in Zendesk and comment in DB when appropriate.

Thank you for calling Bizee. Have a great day!
