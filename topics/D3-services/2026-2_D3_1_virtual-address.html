What It Is

A secure U.S. mailing address that enables customers to receive and manage mail from anywhere in the world. This service helps protect privacy by keeping personal home addresses off public records and eliminates the need to lease physical office space.

Service Overview

All incoming mail is scanned and uploaded to the customer's dashboard, while select physical items, such as credit/debit cards or checks, are forwarded to the contact address on file. We only offer one address location per state in a specific city.

Service Types

We offer two distinct Virtual Address services designed to meet different needs: Personal and Business.

Personal Virtual Address

Intended for individuals such as non-U.S. residents, digital nomads, or remote workers seeking a professional U.S. mailing address for personal mail.

  • One Personal Virtual Address allowed per individual.
  • Mail must be addressed to the individual by their full name as listed on the order.
  • The customer dashboard currently displays business labels for all Virtual Address orders, with the individual's name possibly shown in the "Company" field for personal orders.

Business Virtual Address

Serves companies by providing a professional U.S. mailing address to receive business-related mail, including checks, invoices, bank cards, and government documents.

  • It can be purchased during business formation or as a standalone service.
  • Mail may be addressed to any listed company member or director (but not shareholders, account contacts, or client users).
  • Mail must be addressed to the company name or a listed member.
  • The service is specific to the state where the company is registered and operating.
Standalone Virtual Address Order

For companies not formed through us, the Standalone Virtual Address order allows independent subscription to the service. Customers may also choose to have us update their company address with the Secretary of State, which functions similarly to an Amendment order:

An internal review confirms where the new address applies.
The filing team processes the state amendment once details are confirmed.
USPS 1583

The USPS 1583 form is required whenever using any Virtual Address service within the U.S. It gives us written consent to receive and handle customer's mail for legal purposes, which is required whenever assigning us as the company's CMRA (Commercial Mail Receiving Agent).

Authentication Procedure

The USPS requires all Virtual Address users to complete a Photo ID and Proof of Address verification before Form 1583 can be filled out and signed. At Bizee, this requirement is handled through an integrated (USPS-approved) online verification system called Colleckt, which streamlines the process.

Through Colleckt, customers upload photos of their identification and proof of address documents for review. Once the documents are approved, the customer is prompted to sign Form 1583 directly from the Dashboard. If the submission is not approved, the customer is notified and provided with case-specific guidance explaining the issue and how to correct it. Customers may submit new verification attempts without limitation until the process is completed.

Agents may use available resources in KB, including the instructional video and public access articles containing step-by-step instructions, to assist customers in completing the verification process.

Mail Handling Procedures

To ensure smooth mail reception and processing, the following requirements apply:

Mail must include the full Virtual Address, including Suite or Box number.

Recipient name must match either the business name on file or a listed individual (point of contact, owner, member, or director).

Missing box numbers may cause delays; customers should include them when possible.

Mail addressed to unlisted names will be rejected and returned.

Once received:

Mail is scanned and uploaded to the dashboard, and a notification is sent to the customer. Customers have five business days to request forwarding; otherwise, the mail is destroyed.

The Virtual Address Team will leave a DB Comment every time mail is uploaded or forwarded. Make sure to thoroughly go over the comment section every time you assist a customer with VA mail reception.

Forwarding requires a U.S. contact address. International customers must provide a prepaid shipping label from FedEx, using our Texas address information in the Sender Details section, which will allow us to forward mail abroad.

Changes to Virtual Address Location

Virtual addresses may occasionally change within the same state. If this happens:

Customers receive email notifications when a change occurs.
Previous virtual addresses are documented in the Resource Guide by state.
Bizee typically handles Secretary of State filings for address changes.
Customers must update other providers and agencies themselves.
Reassure customers that mail reception and forwarding continue uninterrupted during location changes.
