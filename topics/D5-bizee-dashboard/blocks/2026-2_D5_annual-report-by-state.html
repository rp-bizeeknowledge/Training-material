Delaware — Franchise Tax Report
LLCs due June 1
Corps & Nonprofits due March 1
1.1.1 Reporting Period

As opposed to other states, Delaware's Franchise Tax Report reflects income data from the previous tax year, not the current. For example:

A Corp formed December 5, 2025 — First report is due March 1, 2026. Report will cover the period from December 5 to December 31, 2025. The dashboard will show "2026 Annual Report" but the PDF file will reflect 2025 activity.

1.1.2 Corporation Tax Calculation

Corporations in DE have to file an Annual Report based on their authorized shares. Depending on how their company is structured, additional fees may be required.

Authorized SharesTax Amount
5,000 shares or less$175.00 (minimum)
5,001 – 10,000 shares$250.00
Each additional 10,000 shares or portion thereof+$85.00
Maximum annual tax$200,000.00

Examples: A corporation with 10,005 shares authorized pays $335.00 ($250.00 plus $85.00). A corporation with 100,000 shares authorized pays $1,015.00 ($250.00 plus $765.00 [$85.00 × 9]).

1.1.3 Internal Handling

Clients might get a pending hold if they input too many shares. This is done to confirm that they want to report that number of shares and that they understand the consequences of it.

If additional fees are required, they will receive a Pending Issue. They will have to provide the information to the Order Issue team to continue with the filing.

If the customer has to pay less than the initial amount, a refund will be applicable to the order. You can confirm this information by reviewing the Annual Report document on Document Upload. There will be a note on DB/Bizee Tech with the refund information.

Pennsylvania — First Annual Report

The first annual report in Pennsylvania is especially significant because it is the first state-issued document that publicly reflects the names of the entity's Members, Directors, or Officers. This should be carefully considered when assisting customers, as the required steps to add, remove, or update these individuals depend on the entity type and whether or not the report has been filed.

1.2.1 LLCs

Articles of Organization list the Members as Organizers, including the names and addresses provided during the order process. Once the first Annual Report is filed, the members will show as Governors with only their names. Organizer's addresses cannot be changed with the state, but updating the members' addresses in the internal documents is possible.

Always check if the first Annual Report has been filed when handling a customer's request to update membership information for a Pennsylvania LLC:

PA LLC — Membership Update Decision

→ First Annual Report has NOT been filed
  • Offer to do an internal update of the members' information (changing names/addresses, adding/removing members).
  • If they're adding or removing a member, let them know the change will only reflect on a state-issued document once the Annual Report is filed.
→ First Annual Report HAS been filed

Adding/removing members can only be done through an Amendment.

1.2.2 C Corps, S Corps & NFPs

Articles of Incorporation list the Directors as Incorporators, including the names and addresses provided during the order process. Once the first Annual Report is filed, the Directors will show as Governors with only their names. Incorporator's addresses cannot be changed with the state, although updating the address for the Directors in the internal documents is possible.

PA Corp/NFP — Governance Update Decision

→ First Annual Report has NOT been filed
  • Offer to do an internal update of the officers'/directors' information (changing names or addresses, adding/removing directors or officers).
  • If they're adding or removing a director/officer, let them know the change will only reflect on a state-issued document once the Annual Report is filed.
  • *Note: Authorized shares cannot be updated internally. Changes can only be made after the first Annual Report is filed.
→ First Annual Report HAS been filed

Adding or removing Directors or Officers, or changing the amount of authorized shares, can only be done through an Amendment.

📌

States do not record shareholder information. C-Corps and S-Corps keep these details in their internal governance documents. S Corporations must also report and update their shareholders with the IRS for tax purposes.

Texas — Franchise Tax & PIR
Annual Report due May 15

In Texas, the Secretary of State handles the formation and public filing of business entities, whereas the Comptroller handles franchise tax, accounting functions, and maintains the tax records for each entity. Because of this, updating business information in Texas may require different processes depending on which agency manages the specific data.

1.3.1 Which Agency Handles What

Secretary of State

  • Company Name
  • Members'/Directors' Names and Addresses
  • Registered Agent

Comptroller

  • Company's Mailing Address
1.3.2 Mailing Address

By default, when we register a Texas company, we list our Registered Agent address as the mailing address. This ensures we receive the Comptroller's welcome letter, which contains the Webfile Number needed for future annual reports. Once received, we upload the letter to the customer's dashboard under State Notifications so they don't miss it. Afterward, customers may update the mailing address directly with the Texas Comptroller if they prefer correspondence sent elsewhere.

1.3.3 Webfile Number

This is a 6-digit alphanumeric number that usually starts with RT, XT, or FQ, and is used to access the Comptroller's online filing access. It gets assigned to a business along with the taxpayer number immediately after its registration in the Secretary of State's records, and is provided through a "Welcome Letter" that is mailed out by the Comptroller a couple of weeks after the company has been formed.

1.3.4 Updating the Comptroller's Records

If a customer wants to update the mailing address after the company has been formed, they must do so directly with the Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts. As a third party, we are not authorized to make changes to the information recorded by the Comptroller on a company's behalf.

🌐

Online: comptroller.texas.gov/web-forms/franchise/changeaddress
By phone: (800) 442-3453
Use the macro "Texas Comptroller Mailing Address Change" to provide customers with detailed instructions. The customer will be required to provide their Webfile Number to complete the address change.

1.3.5 Annual Report (Public Information & Franchise Tax Report)

In Texas, the Annual Report includes two filings: the Franchise Tax Report and the Public Information Report (PIR). The Franchise Tax Report determines whether the business owes franchise tax based on its revenue, while the PIR updates the state's records with current ownership, officer, and registered agent details. Bizee handles both filings whenever a customer orders an Annual Report.

All entity types are required to file their Annual Report each year with the Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts. Both filings are due on May 15, even if no tax is owed. Failure to file may result in the forfeiture of the company's right to do business in Texas.

⚠️

Revenue Threshold: Bizee does not allow Annual Report orders in Texas for businesses reporting revenue of $2.47 million or more. Customers who enter an amount equal to or above this threshold in the order form will see an error screen indicating that the service cannot be processed through our platform.

1.3.4 Sales Tax Registration

In simple terms, sales tax registration in Texas means signing up with the state so you can collect sales tax from customers and send it to Texas. If you sell taxable products or services in Texas, the state wants you to act as the tax collector. Once you register, Texas gives you a sales tax permit that allows you to charge sales tax on taxable sales, file sales tax reports, and send the collected tax to the state. You cannot legally collect Texas sales tax without this permit.

Who needs to register: Businesses that sell physical products in Texas, taxable digital products, taxable services in Texas, businesses with a Texas location, employees, or inventory, or online sellers shipping to Texas customers that pass the economic nexus threshold.

⚠️

Any queries about the Sales Tax Registration should be escalated to your supervisor for review.

Florida — Annual Report High Season
Annual Report due May 1

Florida's Annual Report requirements significantly influence our high season. In 2025, we received over 6,000 Florida Annual Report orders between March and May, as corporations, limited liability companies, and nonprofit organizations are required to file an annual report each year by May 1 to maintain active status.

1.4.1 Fines and Penalties

Reports can be filed beginning on January 1, but filing after May 1 triggers a $400 late fee and may ultimately lead to administrative dissolution if not filed before the third Friday in September. An extra $200 fine is added if this happens.

1.4.2 Internal Handling

The late fee is automatically added at checkout when the filing is close to the May 1st deadline (approximately three days prior). The fee cannot be removed at checkout, but can be refunded if ultimately not needed.

⚠️

You can only process the refund if the Filing Department posted a DB Comment on the order authorizing the specific amount.

⚠️

If applicable, process the refund from the Transaction History tab, selecting "Other" as the refund reason, and typing "Late Fee Refund" in the description field.

California — Statement of Information

In California, the Annual Report is called the Statement of Information (SOI). Every California entity must file an initial SOI within 90 days of formation, as a requirement to maintain good standing.

After the initial filing, LLCs and NFPs are required to file the SOI every two years, while corporations do it every year. The filing window for all entities lasts 6 months and ends on the last day of the company's formation anniversary month.

The name of the company's Members, Directors, or Officers will not become public until the initial SOI is filed.

1.5.1 Franchise Tax Board

The Franchise Tax Board (FTB) handles California business taxes. Most companies formed, registered, or doing business in California must pay a minimum Franchise Tax of $800 each year.

For LLCs, the first $800 payment is due by the 15th day of the 4th month after formation (the formation month counts as month one). Corporations don't pay the $800 minimum tax in the first taxable year, and it typically begins in the second taxable year. Nonprofit corporations may qualify for tax exemption at a state level. If the tax-exempt status is granted, the organization is generally not required to pay the Franchise Tax.

1.5.2 December Order Holds for Corporations
⚠️

If a C Corporation order is placed in early December, the Filing Department will place the order on hold to notify the customer of Franchise Tax Payment implications. The FTB will charge an $800 Franchise Tax to all companies formed before December 17.
