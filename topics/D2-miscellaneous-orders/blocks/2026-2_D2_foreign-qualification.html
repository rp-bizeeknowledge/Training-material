What It Is

A Foreign Qualification is the legal process that allows an existing company to operate in a state other than where it was originally formed. It ensures the business is authorized to operate in both its home state and the new state where it wishes to conduct business. Foreign qualified entities will need to comply with both states' regulations and meet annual reporting and tax obligations.

7.2 — Process
1

Obtain COGS

We obtain the Certificate of Good Standing from the state where the company was originally formed. This ensures the company is in good standing.

2

File Foreign Qualification

We file the Foreign Qualification application with the state where the company wishes to expand. This allows the business to operate as a foreign entity in that state.

7.3 — Special Scenarios
Scenario

Desired Name Not Available in the Foreign State

Issue: The desired business name is not available in the foreign-qualified state.

Solution: The company will need to file for a DBA (Doing Business As) or Assumed Business Name in the foreign state. The company will still operate under its original name but will use a different name in that state.

Action: Gather additional information for the DBA filing in the foreign state.

Scenario

Dissolution or Reinstatement of Foreign Qualification (Withdrawal)

Issue: The customer wants to dissolve or reinstate their Foreign Qualification.

Solution: We do not provide dissolution or reinstatement services for Foreign Qualifications.

Action: Inform the customer that these services are not available for Foreign Qualifications.
