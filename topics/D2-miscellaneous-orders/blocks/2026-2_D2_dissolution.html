Dissolution

Terminating a Business Entity · DB Status Updates · Foreign Qualification Withdrawals
What It Is

A Dissolution refers to the official paperwork required to terminate the existence of a business entity. This process formally notifies the Secretary of State that the company is no longer active, and it helps the business avoid future tax obligations, compliance requirements, or penalties.

5.2 — Foreign Qualification Dissolutions

Dissolutions for Foreign Qualifications are called Withdrawals. We do not offer this service.

5.3 — Updating Company Status in the DB

If you find a company listed as Dissolved, Delinquent, or Revoked on the Secretary of State's website, you must update the company's status in the database (DB) as follows:

State Shows

Delinquent / Revoked / Administratively Dissolved

Update DB To

"Delinquent/Revoked"

State Shows

Voluntarily Dissolved

Update DB To

"Dissolved"

⚠️

Companies that were administratively dissolved by the state must NOT be marked as "Dissolved." This status is reserved only for companies that have voluntarily dissolved. Always differentiate between voluntary and administrative dissolution.

Whenever you update a company's status:

1

Go to the Secretary of State's website and locate the official listing of the company's status.

2

Take a screenshot showing the current status.

3

Upload the screenshot to Internal Storage as documentation and backup for the update you're making in DB.
