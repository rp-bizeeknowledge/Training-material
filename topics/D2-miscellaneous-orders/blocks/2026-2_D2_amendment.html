Amendment

When Needed · EIN Updates · Pre-Formation vs Post · Special Scenarios · Notes
What It Is

An Amendment is a filing used to update a company's official information with the state. It's typically needed when making changes to the business name, address, management, or purpose. Once filed, the updated information becomes part of the public record, so the state and public stay informed.

2.1 — When Is It Needed

Not all business details can be updated through an Amendment. Some information, such as a company's mailing address or shareholder details, is not recorded by the state and should be updated internally in documents like the Operating Agreement or Corporate Bylaws.

An Amendment is only required when the information being changed appears in the state's public records, such as the business name, business activity (NAICS code), or the number of authorized shares, depending on the state. To know whether an Amendment is needed, customers should review their Articles of Incorporation or Organization and compare them with the information they want to change. The state's filing instructions or resource guide can also help confirm what must be amended.

2.2 — Updating an EIN

Sometimes clients think that an amendment will help them change their information with the IRS. In these situations, for changes to a company's name or address, advise customers to contact the IRS directly using the slip at the bottom of their EIN confirmation letter. Bizee cannot update this information on a taxpayer's behalf through an amendment. Changes to an existing EIN need to be done by the responsible party through Form 8821-B.

2.3 — Pre-Formation Changes vs. Amendment Filing

If the order is still processing, an amendment is usually not needed. We can apply the requested changes before the documents are submitted to the state. If the change cannot be made, the order needs to be placed again.

If the documents have been filed, an amendment will be required to make any changes to state-registered information.

2.4 — SOS Website Updates

Inform customers that Secretary of State (SoS) websites are not updated daily. Once the information has been updated, the changes will reflect on the website. Customers should allow some time for the update to be reflected.

2.5 — Special Scenarios

Delaware

Delaware allows customers to decide whether or not to list company members. If a customer initially chose not to list members or the company address, they can file an amendment later to add this information.

Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania doesn't show members on the Articles until the first Annual Report has been filed. If customers want to change this information, they will have to wait until the report's deadline is near so the change can be made through an Amendment.

Updating DBA Address After Company Address Amendment

When a customer files an amendment to change the company address with the Secretary of State, the DBA (Doing Business As) address does not automatically update. The customer will need to update the DBA address directly with the county where the DBA was initially filed.

2.6 — Notes

Occasionally, shares are presented with a par value, which may require an amendment for any change. This is commonly seen in states like Wyoming. It's important to note that shareholder details are never disclosed in any state's public records.

If shareholder information is not listed with the state or has already been amended, updates can be made upon request via email from the customer.

An amendment allows for multiple changes to be made at once. You can change the company name, add or remove a member, and update the address all in a single amendment. It is not limited to one change per amendment.

2.7 — Filing Timeframes

We provide an estimated turnaround time for Amendments in most states, with an Expedited Processing option available at checkout. Always follow the same steps a customer would take to place the order from the dashboard, to confirm the exact timeframe being shown at that moment.
